## Residential Code

**Adopted**: IRC 2021 with Utah amendments

### Key Amendments
- **Sprinklers**: Not required statewide
- **Radon**: Zone 1 (northern Utah / Wasatch Front) — active radon mitigation system recommended; passive required by many jurisdictions
- **Seismic**: Enhanced hold-down requirements Wasatch Front — SDC C-D
- **Wildfire**: WUI code applies in Wasatch foothills and southern Utah
- **Frost depth**: 30" Salt Lake City; 36-42" mountain communities

---

## Seismic / Wind / Natural Hazards

### Seismic (Critical — Wasatch Front)
| Region | Typical SDC | Notes |
|--------|-------------|-------|
| Salt Lake City / Wasatch Front | C-D | Wasatch Fault — M7.0+ potential |
| Ogden / Provo | C-D | High risk urban area |
| St. George | B-C | Hurricane Fault zone |
| Rural eastern Utah | A-B | Low risk |

- **Wasatch Fault**: ~240-mile fault — Salt Lake City segment capable of M7.0-7.5 earthquake
- **Liquefaction**: Significant risk in former lake sediments (Lake Bonneville) — most of Salt Lake valley
- **Landslide**: Wasatch foothill slopes — geotechnical investigation required
- **Special inspections**: Required SDC C-D

### Wind
- Salt Lake Valley: 90-100 mph design
- Mountain ridges: 100-115 mph
- St. George / Moab: 90-100 mph

### Wildfire
- Wasatch foothills (east bench communities): High WUI risk
- Southern Utah: Cedar City, St. George foothills
- Class A roofing, ember-resistant vents in WUI zones

### Snow
| Region | Ground Snow Load |
|--------|----------------|
| St. George | 5-10 psf |
| Salt Lake City valley | 30 psf |
| Park City / Cottonwood Canyons | 100-150 psf |
| High Uintas | 200+ psf |

## Notable State Amendments
- **Wasatch Front seismic**: One of highest-risk urban areas in US — design beyond minimums
- **Liquefaction**: Geotech report strongly recommended for Wasatch Front projects
- **Radon**: Zone 1 throughout most of northern Utah — always include mitigation system
- **Salt Lake City local codes**: SLC has local amendments — verify with city
