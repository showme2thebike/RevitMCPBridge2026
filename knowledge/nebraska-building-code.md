# Nebraska Building Code - Key Amendments

**Based on**: Nebraska Building Code, adopting IBC 2018
**Base Code**: IBC 2018 with Nebraska amendments
**Authority**: Nebraska State Building Division (Office of the State Fire Marshal for state-owned buildings); local AHJs govern municipalities

## Energy Code

**Adopted**: IECC 2018

**Climate Zones by Region**:
| Region | Climate Zone | Cities |
|--------|-------------|--------|
| North / Omaha metro | 5A (cool-humid) | Omaha, Fremont, Norfolk, Columbus |
| South / Lincoln | 4A (mixed-humid) | Lincoln, Beatrice, Nebraska City |
| Panhandle / West | 5B (cool-dry) | Scottsbluff, Alliance, North Platte |

**Zone 5A Residential Envelope Minimums (IECC 2018)**:
- Ceiling/attic: R-49
- Wood-frame walls: R-20 or R-13 + R-5ci
- Floors over unconditioned space: R-30
- Slab edge: R-10 for 2 ft
- Windows: U-0.30, SHGC no requirement

**Zone 4A Residential Envelope Minimums (IECC 2018)**:
- Ceiling/attic: R-49
- Wood-frame walls: R-20 or R-13 + R-5ci
- Floors over unconditioned space: R-19
- Slab edge: R-10 for 2 ft
- Windows: U-0.32, SHGC 0.40

**Zone 5B Residential Envelope Minimums (IECC 2018)**:
- Ceiling/attic: R-49
- Wood-frame walls: R-20 or R-13 + R-5ci
- Floors over unconditioned space: R-30
- Slab edge: R-10 for 2 ft
- Windows: U-0.30, SHGC no requirement (dry climate — solar gain beneficial)

**Key Energy Notes**:
- Air sealing required; blower-door or visual inspection at rough-in
- Duct leakage testing: postconstruction ≤4 CFM25 per 100 sq ft CFA
- Vapor retarder: Class I or II on warm-in-winter side required in Zones 4–5
- ASHRAE 62.2 mechanical ventilation for tight envelopes

## Residential Code

**Adopted**: IRC 2018

**Key IRC Provisions for Nebraska**:
- Minimum ceiling height: 7'-0" habitable space
- Stair riser max: 7-3/4", tread min: 10"
- Egress window: 5.7 sq ft net clear, 20" wide min, 24" high min, sill max 44" AFF
- Smoke alarms: each sleeping room, outside each sleeping area, each story
- Carbon monoxide alarms: required with fuel-fired appliances or attached garage
- Garage separation: 1/2" gypsum on garage-side wall and ceiling

**Residential Sprinklers**:
- No statewide mandate for one- and two-family dwellings
- Omaha and some larger cities may have local amendments — verify with local AHJ

## Seismic / Wind / Natural Hazards

### Seismic

Nebraska is in a region of **low seismic hazard**.

| Region | Seismic Design Category | Notes |
|--------|------------------------|-------|
| Statewide | SDC A | No special seismic detailing required |

- SDC A: Standard construction per IRC/IBC without seismic-specific detailing
- No fault zones; no induced seismicity hazard of note

### Wind Design — CRITICAL

**Nebraska lies in Tornado Alley. Wind design is the dominant structural concern. The state has some of the highest design wind speeds in the nation due to extreme open-terrain exposure.**

| Region | Basic Wind Speed (Vult) | Exposure |
|--------|------------------------|----------|
| Panhandle / Western NE | 115–120 mph | Exposure C (open prairie) |
| Central Nebraska | 115–120 mph | Exposure C common |
| Eastern NE / Omaha | 115 mph | Exposure B (suburban) or C |

**Open terrain (Exposure C) dramatically increases design wind pressure** — most of rural Nebraska defaults to Exposure C.

**Design Requirements**:
- ASCE 7-16 wind load analysis
- Roof-to-wall straps/hurricane ties: strongly recommended, increasingly required locally
- Gable end bracing required for wood-frame construction
- Garage doors: pressure-rated per wind design; overhead door manufacturer rating required
- Roofing: enhanced nailing schedules at high wind zones

**Tornado Shelter**:
- No statewide mandate for residential tornado shelters
- ICC 500 and FEMA P-361 are applicable design standards
- FEMA grant programs available; consult local emergency management
- Lincoln and Omaha have local safe room incentive programs

### Snow Loads

Nebraska experiences significant snow loads, especially in the north.

| Region | Ground Snow Load (Pg) |
|--------|----------------------|
| Northern Nebraska (Norfolk, Norfolk) | 25–30 psf |
| Omaha area | 25 psf |
| Lincoln / central | 20–25 psf |
| Panhandle / Scottsbluff | 20–30 psf (drifting significant) |
| Southern Nebraska | 20 psf |

- Unbalanced loads and drifting per ASCE 7 Chapter 7
- Low-slope roofs in heavy snow areas need careful drainage design

### Radon — Zone 1 Statewide

**Nebraska has one of the highest radon risks in the nation. The entire state is EPA Radon Zone 1 (predicted average indoor radon >4 pCi/L).**

**Radon-Resistant New Construction (RRNC)**:
- Required by strong state guidance; some jurisdictions have made it mandatory in permits
- Components:
  1. 4-inch gravel sub-slab gas permeable layer (or alternative)
  2. 6-mil polyethylene vapor barrier on sub-slab gravel
  3. All sub-slab penetrations sealed
  4. 3" or 4" PVC passive vent pipe from sub-slab to above roof
  5. Electrical outlet rough-in in attic near vent for future active fan
- Post-construction radon test recommended before occupancy
- If test >4 pCi/L: install inline vent fan (active mitigation)

**Nebraska Radon Program**: Nebraska DHHS Radon Program provides free test kits

## Notable State Amendments

1. **No statewide residential sprinkler mandate** — IRC R313 not adopted at state level
2. **Radon Zone 1 statewide** — passive radon system is de facto required on every residential project; document on drawings
3. **Adoption lag**: Nebraska generally follows IBC 2018/IRC 2018 but local jurisdictions vary; Lincoln and Omaha sometimes lead with local amendments
4. **Local jurisdiction authority**: Nebraska cities have broad authority to adopt stricter codes; Omaha, Lincoln, and other cities may have local amendments
5. **State buildings**: Nebraska State Building Division has jurisdiction over state-owned buildings; local AHJ governs private construction
6. **Frost depth**: Northern Nebraska 36–42 inches; Omaha 36 inches; western/panhandle 36–40 inches — footings must go below frost line
7. **Manufactured/modular housing**: Governed by Nebraska Manufactured Housing Division under DHHS

## Quick Checklist - New Residential

- [ ] Confirm climate zone (4A, 5A, or 5B) for project location
- [ ] IECC 2018 envelope values applied — ceiling, walls, floor, slab, windows
- [ ] Vapor retarder (Class I or II) on warm-in-winter side per Zone 5 requirement
- [ ] Air sealing and duct leakage testing method selected
- [ ] Radon-resistant construction (RRNC) designed — gravel, vapor barrier, vent pipe, outlet rough-in
- [ ] Frost depth confirmed with local AHJ (36–42" range statewide)
- [ ] Wind speed (Vult) and exposure category determined (Exposure C common in rural NE)
- [ ] Roof-to-wall connections (hurricane straps) per wind analysis
- [ ] Garage door wind pressure rating confirmed
- [ ] Snow load per ASCE 7 Chapter 7; drifting evaluated for complex roof forms
- [ ] Tornado safe room: discuss with owner; document FEMA P-361 if provided
- [ ] Smoke and CO alarms per IRC 2018
- [ ] Egress windows per IRC R310 in all sleeping rooms
- [ ] Verify local AHJ's adopted code edition and amendments
