# Arizona Building Code - Key Amendments

**Based on**: Arizona adopts IBC 2018 at state level; local adoption varies
**Base Code**: IBC 2018 with Arizona amendments
**Authority**: Arizona Department of Fire, Building and Life Safety (DFBLS); local jurisdictions

---

## Quick Reference - Arizona vs. IBC

| Requirement | Arizona | IBC 2021 |
|-------------|---------|----------|
| Code edition | IBC 2018 (varies locally) | IBC 2021 |
| Energy code | IECC 2018 | IECC 2021 |
| Residential sprinklers | Not required statewide | Not required |
| Seismic | SDC A-B | Per ASCE 7 |
| Wildfire | WUI provisions (northern AZ) | Not addressed |
| Extreme heat | Design consideration | Not addressed |

## Energy Code

**Adopted**: IECC 2018
**Climate Zones**: 2B (Phoenix/Tucson area), 3B (Prescott/Flagstaff transition), 4B/5B (Flagstaff)

| Element | Zone 2B | Zone 3B | Zone 5B |
|---------|---------|---------|---------|
| Ceiling | R-38 | R-38 | R-49 |
| Wall | R-13 | R-13 | R-20 |
| Floor | R-13 | R-19 | R-30 |
| Windows | U-0.40 | U-0.35 | U-0.30 |
| SHGC | 0.25 | 0.25 | 0.40 |

**Note**: SHGC (solar heat gain coefficient) is critical in hot climates — limits solar gain through glass.

## Residential Code

**Adopted**: IRC 2018 (locally adopted — Phoenix, Tucson, Scottsdale may differ)

### Key Amendments
- **Sprinklers**: Not required statewide
- **Extreme heat**: 110-118°F design temperature Phoenix — HVAC sizing critical
- **Adobe/masonry**: Traditional construction materials; earthen construction provisions
- **Frost depth**: Minimal — 6-12" northern AZ; 0" southern AZ
- **Termite**: Required — Arizona has significant subterranean termite pressure (desert species)

---

## Seismic / Wind / Natural Hazards

### Seismic
- Statewide SDC A-B — low seismic risk
- Standard IBC provisions apply
- Some faulting in Basin and Range terrain

### Wind / Dust Storms
- **Haboobs**: Massive dust storms in Phoenix metro — 50-60 mph winds with near-zero visibility
- **Monsoon**: July-September — severe thunderstorms, flash flooding, wind gusts 60+ mph
- **Design wind**: 90-100 mph statewide
- **Dust infiltration**: Seal all penetrations carefully

### Wildfire (Northern Arizona)
- **High risk**: Coconino, Yavapai, Navajo counties (Flagstaff, Prescott, Show Low)
- **WUI Code**: Applies in designated interface areas
- **Class A roofing**: Required WUI zones
- **2002 Show Low Fire, 2011 Wallow Fire**: Largest in state history — ongoing WUI risk

### Flooding
- **Flash flooding**: Critical concern — desert soils cannot absorb intense monsoon rain
- **100-year events**: Occur regularly; FEMA FIRM zones important
- **Washes**: Many Arizona communities built near ephemeral washes — regulated setbacks
- **Maricopa County Flood Control**: Extensive channelization and basin system

### Extreme Heat
- Phoenix record: 118°F (2020)
- **Radiant barriers**: Highly recommended in attics — reduce cooling load 10-20%
- **Concrete slabs**: Early morning pours in summer; reflective coatings
- **Exterior materials**: Light-colored, heat-resistant required

---

## Notable State Amendments
- **Phoenix metro**: Maricopa County Flood Control District has own permits for wash-adjacent projects
- **Tucson**: Water harvesting requirements — cisterns for rainwater collection
- **Scottsdale**: McDowell Sonoran Preserve setbacks; dark sky lighting requirements
- **Flagstaff**: Dark sky city; all exterior lighting must be shielded downward
- **HOA rules**: Very common in AZ — often more restrictive than code for aesthetics

## Quick Checklist - New Residential
- [ ] Climate zone (2B Phoenix / 3B Prescott / 5B Flagstaff)
- [ ] SHGC — solar heat gain control for windows
- [ ] Radiant barrier in attic
- [ ] Extreme heat — HVAC sizing (Manual J)
- [ ] Termite protection specified
- [ ] Flood / wash setback — Maricopa County FCD permit?
- [ ] WUI zone? (northern AZ)
- [ ] Monsoon drainage — grading away from structure critical
