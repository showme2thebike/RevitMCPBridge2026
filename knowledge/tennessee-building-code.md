## Residential Code

**Adopted**: IRC 2018 with Tennessee amendments

### Key Amendments
- **Sprinklers**: Not required statewide for 1-2 family
- **Seismic**: Enhanced hold-down requirements Memphis area — New Madrid Seismic Zone
- **Frost depth**: 8-12" (minimal frost in most of TN)
- **Termite**: Required — significant termite pressure statewide
- **Crawl space**: Vapor barrier required; encapsulation recommended

---

## Seismic / Wind / Natural Hazards

### Seismic (Critical — Memphis Area)
| Region | Typical SDC | Notes |
|--------|-------------|-------|
| Memphis / Shelby County | C-D | New Madrid Seismic Zone — M7+ potential |
| West Tennessee | B-C | Elevated risk |
| Nashville / Middle TN | A-B | Standard |
| East Tennessee | A-B | Standard |

- **New Madrid Seismic Zone**: One of highest seismic risks east of Rockies — M7.0-8.0 scenario
- **Memphis**: SDC C-D; special inspections required; unreinforced masonry highly vulnerable
- **Liquefaction**: Significant risk along Mississippi River corridor

### Wind / Tornado
- **Tornado risk**: Very high — Tennessee averages 20-25 tornadoes annually
- **Tornado Alley extension**: West Tennessee particularly exposed
- **Design wind**: 90-100 mph statewide; no enhanced tornado construction provisions
- Nashville March 2020 tornado (EF3) — major urban tornado event

### Flooding
- FEMA flood zones along Cumberland, Tennessee, Mississippi rivers
- Nashville: Significant 2010 flood event — AE floodplain management important
- TVA lakes and reservoirs — shoreline regulation

## Notable State Amendments
- **Memphis seismic risk**: Often underestimated — design proactively for SDC C-D
- **Nashville growth**: Rapidly growing; verify current local amendments
- **Knoxville/TVA**: TVA shoreline management for lakefront properties
- **Manufactured housing**: DCI regulates separately
