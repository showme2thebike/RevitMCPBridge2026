**Based on**: Delaware Building Code, based on IBC 2018
**Base Code**: IBC 2018 with Delaware amendments
**Authority**: Delaware Office of State Fire Marshal; county/local jurisdictions for private construction

## Residential Code

**Adopted**: IRC 2018 — locally adopted; verify with county AHJ

### Key Amendments
- **Sprinklers**: Not required statewide for 1-2 family
- **Coastal**: Sussex County (Rehoboth Beach, Lewes, Bethany Beach) — FEMA flood zones; NFIP compliance critical
- **Radon**: Zone 2 statewide — passive system recommended; some local jurisdictions require
- **Frost depth**: 36" statewide
- **Wind-borne debris**: Coastal communities — impact glazing or shutters per local adoption

---

## Seismic / Wind / Natural Hazards

### Seismic
- SDC B statewide — moderate risk
- Standard provisions apply

### Wind / Hurricane
| Region | Design Wind Speed | Notes |
|--------|-----------------|-------|
| Sussex County coast | 115-120 mph | Atlantic coast exposure |
| Rehoboth / Lewes | 115 mph | Direct coastal |
| Wilmington / north DE | 100-105 mph | |

- Post-Sandy (2012): FEMA maps updated; Delaware coast — VE/AE zones expanded
- **Delmarva Peninsula**: Low-lying — storm surge flooding significant even with weaker storms

### Flooding
- **Coastal**: Low-lying barrier beaches — storm surge critical
- **Inland**: Christina River, Brandywine Creek — flash flooding in Wilmington
- Chesapeake Bay influence on western Sussex County

---

## Notable State Amendments
- **Sussex County**: Beach resort construction high-volume; verify local AHJ (Sussex County Code Enforcement)
- **New Castle County / Wilmington**: Strongest local enforcement; urban building stock
- **Kent County / Dover**: State capital — moderate local enforcement
- **DNREC**: Delaware Dept of Natural Resources and Environmental Control — coastal zone permits required
