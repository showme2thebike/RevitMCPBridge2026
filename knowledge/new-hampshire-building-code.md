## Energy Code

**Adopted**: IECC 2018
**Climate Zones**: 6A (most of NH — Manchester, Concord, Nashua), 7 (northern NH — White Mountains, Pittsburg/North Country)

| Element | Zone 6A | Zone 7 |
|---------|---------|--------|
| Ceiling | R-60 | R-60 |
| Wall | R-20+5ci | R-20+5ci |
| Floor | R-38 | R-38 |
| Windows | U-0.25 | U-0.20 |
| Air sealing | 3.0 ACH50 | 3.0 ACH50 |

### New Hampshire Energy Notes
- **Design temperature**: -10°F to -25°F (North Country / northern NH)
- **HRV/ERV**: Essential for tight construction — heating season exceeds 7 months in north
- **Heating fuel**: Oil historically dominant; propane rural; heat pumps viable in Zone 6A south

## Residential Code

**Adopted**: New Hampshire Residential Code, based on IRC 2018

### Key Amendments
- **Sprinklers**: Not required statewide for 1-2 family
- **Radon**: **Zone 1 throughout most of NH** — granite bedrock statewide creates very high radon potential; passive system strongly recommended; NH DES (Dept of Environmental Services) testing widely encouraged
- **Snow**: Structural design absolutely critical — White Mountains among heaviest in eastern US
- **Frost depth**: 60" (White Mountains / northern NH); 48" southern NH (Manchester/Nashua)
- **Ice dam**: Ice and water shield mandatory — 24" from exterior wall; cold climate air sealing essential

## Seismic / Wind / Natural Hazards

### Seismic
- SDC B southern NH — moderate risk
- Wentworth/Seabrook area has had moderate seismic events
- **Seabrook Nuclear Station**: Nearby — no additional construction requirements for most projects but worth awareness

### Snow (Critical — White Mountains)
| Region | Ground Snow Load |
|--------|----------------|
| Nashua / Manchester | 50-60 psf |
| Concord / Lakes Region | 60-70 psf |
| Plymouth / Conway | 70-90 psf |
| White Mountain valleys | 80-100 psf |
| Mount Washington summit | 100+ psf |

- **Drift loads**: Critical at roof level changes, parapets, valleys — White Mountains terrain creates severe drift conditions
- **Ice storms**: Frequent — design for ice accumulation on roofs and overhangs

### Wind
- **Mount Washington**: Holds world record for surface wind gust (231 mph, 1934)
- Valley design wind: 90-100 mph; mountain ridges much higher
- Nor'easters: Significant coastal and mountain events

### Coastal (Short Coast — Portsmouth / Hampton area)
- 18-mile coast with Atlantic Ocean exposure
- Design wind 100-105 mph
- Sandy (2012) affected Hampton Beach area with flooding

## Notable State Amendments
- **Manchester / Nashua**: Largest cities; strong local enforcement
- **Conway / North Conway**: Mount Washington Valley — resort construction; heavy snow design critical
- **Seacoast area (Portsmouth)**: Historic and resort construction; coastal flood awareness
- **NH DES**: State environmental permits for wetlands, shoreline; NH Shoreland Protection Act
- **White Mountains National Forest**: Federal projects under USFS oversight
