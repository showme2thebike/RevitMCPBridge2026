## Residential Code

**Adopted**: Alabama Residential Code, based on IRC 2018

### Key Amendments
- **Sprinklers**: Not required for 1-2 family statewide
- **Termite**: Mandatory statewide — soil treatment and barriers required; Formosan termite significant in southern AL
- **Hurricane**: Coastal counties — wind-borne debris region; impact glazing or shutters
- **Frost depth**: Minimal — 6-12" north AL; essentially zero south AL
- **Crawl space**: Vapor barrier required; ventilation or encapsulation per IRC

---

## Seismic / Wind / Natural Hazards

### Seismic
- SDC A statewide — very low risk
- Standard provisions apply

### Tornado (Critical)
- **Dixie Alley**: Alabama is one of the highest tornado-risk states in the US
- **2011 Super Outbreak (April 27)**: 252 tornadoes in Alabama alone — deadliest single tornado day in state history; 249 killed
- **2011 Joplin-class events**: Birmingham metro, Tuscaloosa directly hit
- **Average**: 25-30 tornadoes/year; significantly higher in severe years
- No statewide enhanced tornado construction provisions

### Wind / Hurricane
| Region | Design Wind Speed | Notes |
|--------|-----------------|-------|
| Mobile / coast | 130 mph | Hurricane Ivan 2004 direct hit |
| Baldwin County coast | 130 mph | Storm surge critical |
| Birmingham / inland | 100-105 mph | |
| North Alabama | 100 mph | |

- **Hurricane Ivan (2004)**: Category 3 landfall near Gulf Shores — extensive damage
- **Wind-borne debris region**: Within 1 mile of coast and areas ≥110 mph
- **Mobile Bay**: Storm surge amplifies coastal flooding

### Flooding
- Coastal: Mobile Bay and barrier island surge flooding
- Inland: Flash flooding river systems (Black Warrior, Cahaba, Alabama Rivers)

## Notable State Amendments
- **Termite mandatory**: Pretreatment required before slab pour; final inspection documentation
- **Mobile**: City has local building department enforcement; verify AHJ
- **Birmingham**: Jefferson County has strong local code enforcement
- **Gulf Shores / Orange Beach**: Resort coastal construction; strict wind requirements
- **ADEM**: Alabama Dept of Environmental Management for coastal/wetland permits
