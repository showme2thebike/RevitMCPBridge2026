**Base Code**: IBC 2015 with Michigan amendments
**Authority**: Michigan Department of Licensing and Regulatory Affairs (LARA), Bureau of Construction Codes

## Residential Code

**Adopted**: Michigan Residential Code, based on IRC 2015

### Key Amendments
- **Sprinklers**: Not required statewide for 1-2 family
- **Radon**: Zone 1 throughout most of state — passive radon system strongly recommended; active in high-risk areas
- **Snow**: See table below — lake-effect belt extreme
- **Frost depth**: 42" Lower Peninsula; 48-60" Upper Peninsula
- **Basement walls**: Full perimeter drainage required (high water table common)

---

## Seismic / Wind / Natural Hazards

### Seismic
- SDC A statewide — extremely low risk
- Standard provisions apply

### Snow (Critical — especially UP)
| Region | Ground Snow Load |
|--------|----------------|
| Detroit / SE Michigan | 25-30 psf |
| Grand Rapids / West MI | 30-40 psf |
| Traverse City area | 40-50 psf |
| Marquette / Keweenaw (UP) | 100-150 psf |
| Houghton County (UP) | 150-200 psf |

- **Lake-effect**: Lake Superior and Lake Michigan generate extreme localized snowfall
- **Keweenaw Peninsula**: Among highest snowfall in eastern US (200-300" annually)
- **Structural engineer**: Always required for UP projects; recommended west lower peninsula
- **Drift loads**: Critical at roof level changes, parapets

### Wind
- Great Lakes coast: 90-100 mph
- Statewide: 85-100 mph
- Tornado: Moderate — lower peninsula averages 15-20/year
- No enhanced statewide tornado provisions

### Flooding
- Great Lakes shoreline erosion (dynamic)
- FEMA flood zones along major rivers (Grand, Kalamazoo, Muskegon)
- High water table — basement flooding common in low-lying areas

## Notable State Amendments
- **Detroit/Wayne County**: Local amendments; verify with building department
- **Radon**: Upper peninsula particularly high — always include passive system statewide
- **Grand Rapids**: Growing market; verify current local edition
- **Water table**: High in many areas — waterproofing and drainage system critical
- **MBC vs. local**: Most cities in Michigan use MBC with minimal local amendments

## Quick Checklist - New Residential
- [ ] Climate zone (5A lower / 6A upper peninsula)
- [ ] Snow load — UP requires structural engineer; verify design load
- [ ] Frost depth (42" LP / 48-60" UP)
- [ ] Radon passive system (Zone 1 — statewide)
- [ ] Basement drainage — perimeter drain system
- [ ] Great Lakes shoreline? Coastal erosion setbacks
- [ ] Lake-effect belt? Enhanced snow drift design
