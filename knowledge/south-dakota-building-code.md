# South Dakota Building Code - Key Amendments

**Based on**: South Dakota Codified Laws Title 11, adopting IBC 2018
**Base Code**: IBC 2018 with South Dakota amendments
**Authority**: South Dakota State Fire Marshal (state buildings and life safety); local AHJs for municipalities; county authority for unincorporated areas

## Energy Code

**Adopted**: IECC 2018

**Climate Zones by Region**:
| Region | Climate Zone | Cities |
|--------|-------------|--------|
| Eastern SD (Sioux Falls, Brookings) | 6A (cold-humid) | Sioux Falls, Brookings, Watertown, Aberdeen |
| Western SD / Black Hills | 5B (cool-dry) | Rapid City, Spearfish, Hot Springs |

**Zone 6A Residential Envelope Minimums (IECC 2018)**:
- Ceiling/attic: R-49
- Wood-frame walls: R-20 + R-5ci or R-13 + R-10ci
- Floors over unconditioned space: R-30
- Slab edge: R-15 for 4 ft
- Windows: U-0.30, SHGC no requirement

**Zone 5B Residential Envelope Minimums (IECC 2018)**:
- Ceiling/attic: R-49
- Wood-frame walls: R-20 or R-13 + R-5ci
- Floors over unconditioned space: R-30
- Slab edge: R-10 for 2 ft
- Windows: U-0.30, SHGC no requirement (dry climate — solar gain can be beneficial)

**Key Energy Notes**:
- Vapor retarder (Class I) on warm-in-winter side required in Zone 6A
- Air leakage: ≤3.0 ACH50 at blower door test
- Zone 5B (western SD): drier climate means less vapor drive concern; drainage plane and bulk water management more important
- Mechanical ventilation (HRV recommended in Zone 6A)
- Duct leakage testing required

## Residential Code

**Adopted**: IRC 2018

**Key IRC Provisions for South Dakota**:
- Minimum ceiling height: 7'-0" habitable space
- Stair riser max: 7-3/4", tread min: 10"
- Egress window: 5.7 sq ft net clear, 20" wide min, 24" high min, sill max 44" AFF
- Smoke alarms: each sleeping room, outside each sleeping area, each story
- Carbon monoxide alarms: required with fuel-fired appliances or attached garage
- Garage separation: 1/2" gypsum on garage-side of wall and ceiling

**Residential Sprinklers**:
- No statewide mandate for one- and two-family dwellings
- Some municipalities may have local requirements — always verify with local AHJ

## Seismic / Wind / Natural Hazards

### Seismic

South Dakota has generally low seismic risk, with slightly elevated risk in the Black Hills.

| Region | Seismic Design Category | Notes |
|--------|------------------------|-------|
| Eastern SD | SDC A | No special seismic detailing |
| Western SD / Black Hills | SDC A–B | Slightly elevated; ancient basement rock |
| Rapid City area | SDC B | Standard IRC braced wall and anchor requirements apply |

- Most residential construction: standard IRC prescriptive is adequate
- SDC B: Anchor bolts and braced wall panels per IRC Chapter 6

### Wind Design

**South Dakota is at the northern edge of Tornado Alley and experiences extreme open-terrain wind exposure across the eastern plains.**

| Region | Basic Wind Speed (Vult) | Exposure |
|--------|------------------------|----------|
| Eastern SD (Sioux Falls) | 100–110 mph | B/C |
| Central SD (Pierre) | 105–115 mph | C (very flat, open) |
| Western SD (Rapid City) | 100–110 mph | B/C; terrain varies |
| Black Hills (elevated terrain) | 100–115 mph | B; terrain sheltering varies |

**Design Requirements**:
- ASCE 7-16 wind design
- Exposure C default for rural eastern SD; verify for suburban/urban sites
- Roof-to-wall connections and hurricane straps recommended
- Gable end bracing for wood-frame construction
- Garage door pressure ratings per local wind speed

**Tornado Risk**:
- Tornadoes occur, particularly in eastern SD during spring/summer
- No statewide residential tornado shelter mandate
- FEMA P-361 / ICC 500 applicable; FEMA grant programs available

### Snow Loads — Significant

| Region | Ground Snow Load (Pg) |
|--------|----------------------|
| Eastern SD (Sioux Falls, Brookings) | 35–45 psf |
| Aberdeen / northeast SD | 40–50 psf |
| Pierre / central | 25–30 psf |
| Rapid City / western | 20–30 psf |
| Black Hills (elevations) | 50–80+ psf (elevation-dependent) |

**Critical Snow Design Issues**:
- Black Hills: elevation dramatically increases snow load; site-specific snow study may be needed at higher elevations
- Drift loads per ASCE 7 at roof level changes and adjacent to parapets
- Unbalanced loads on sloped roofs
- Ice dams at eaves in Zone 6A — proper insulation and air sealing critical

### Extreme Temperature Range

**South Dakota experiences one of the most extreme temperature ranges in the nation: -40°F winter lows to 110°F summer highs.**

This creates significant design implications:
- Thermal expansion/contraction of all materials — joint sizing, sealant selection critical
- Foundation design: frost heave and soil shrinkage cycles
- Mechanical systems: heating system capacity for extreme cold; cooling often needed too
- Exterior cladding: materials must tolerate extreme thermal cycling
- Air conditioning: often assumed unnecessary in northern climates, but 110°F events make cooling important for habitability
- Energy modeling: both heating and cooling loads matter; balanced design

### Frost Depth

| Region | Minimum Frost Depth |
|--------|-------------------|
| Sioux Falls / southeastern | 42–48 inches |
| Brookings / northeast | 48–54 inches |
| Aberdeen / north-central | 54–60 inches |
| Pierre / central | 48–54 inches |
| Rapid City / western | 42–48 inches |
| Black Hills | Varies with elevation; 36–48 inches at lower elevations |

- Always confirm frost depth with local AHJ
- Heated structures: insulated slab edge per IECC
- Unheated structures (garages): frost-protected shallow foundation (FPSF) or footings below frost line

### Radon — Zone 1 Statewide

**All of South Dakota is EPA Radon Zone 1 (highest risk — predicted average >4 pCi/L).**

**Radon-Resistant New Construction**:
- 4" gravel sub-slab gas-permeable layer
- 6-mil polyethylene vapor barrier on sub-slab gravel
- All sub-slab penetrations sealed
- 3" or 4" PVC passive vent pipe from sub-slab to above roof
- Electrical junction box in attic near vent pipe for future active fan
- Post-construction test before occupancy recommended; active fan if >4 pCi/L

## Notable State Amendments

1. **No statewide residential sprinkler mandate** — IRC R313 not adopted at state level
2. **Radon Zone 1 statewide** — RRNC is standard practice on every residential project
3. **Black Hills special considerations**: Higher elevation = higher snow loads and seismicity; mountain terrain changes wind exposure; verify all loads with local AHJ and local snow study
4. **Extreme temperature range** — material specification and joint design must account for -40°F to 110°F range
5. **Local authority**: South Dakota cities have broad discretion; Sioux Falls and Rapid City have active building departments with local amendments; confirm adoption status
6. **Agricultural exemptions**: Many agricultural buildings in rural SD have reduced code requirements; residential within ag operations still requires full residential compliance
7. **Frost-Protected Shallow Foundations (FPSF)**: Common in SD per IRC Appendix F; confirm with AHJ

## Quick Checklist - New Residential

- [ ] Confirm climate zone (6A east or 5B west) for project location
- [ ] IECC 2018 envelope values applied per zone
- [ ] Frost depth confirmed with local AHJ (42–60" range)
- [ ] Foundation designed below frost; slab edge insulated per IECC
- [ ] Radon-resistant construction designed — gravel, vapor barrier, vent pipe, attic outlet
- [ ] Vapor retarder (Class I) on warm-in-winter side (Zone 6A)
- [ ] Snow load confirmed per ASCE 7; Black Hills elevation study if needed
- [ ] Drift loads at roof transitions evaluated
- [ ] Ice dam prevention: attic insulation, air sealing, ventilation (Zone 6A)
- [ ] Heating capacity for extreme cold (-40°F design range in north); cooling considered
- [ ] Thermal expansion: material and joint selection for extreme temperature range
- [ ] Wind speed (Vult) and exposure category determined
- [ ] Tornado shelter: discuss with owner; FEMA P-361 if provided
- [ ] Smoke and CO alarms per IRC 2018
- [ ] Egress windows per IRC R310 in all sleeping rooms
- [ ] Verify local AHJ adopted code edition and local amendments
