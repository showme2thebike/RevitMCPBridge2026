## Energy Code

**Adopted**: IECC 2015
**Climate Zones**: 2A (Gulf Coast / south MS — Biloxi, Gulfport, Hattiesburg), 3A (central-north MS — Jackson, Oxford)

| Element | Zone 2A | Zone 3A |
|---------|---------|---------|
| Ceiling | R-30 | R-38 |
| Wall | R-13 | R-13 |
| Floor | R-13 | R-19 |
| Windows | U-0.40, SHGC 0.25 | U-0.35 |

### Mississippi Energy Notes
- **Cooling dominant**: High humidity + heat — SHGC control critical on Gulf Coast
- **Humidity**: Dehumidification design important; HVAC sizing for latent load

## Residential Code

**Adopted**: Mississippi Residential Code, based on IRC 2018

### Key Amendments
- **Sprinklers**: Not required statewide for 1-2 family
- **Termite**: **Mandatory statewide** — Formosan termite is catastrophic along Gulf Coast; pretreatment required before slab pour
- **Hurricane**: Coast — 3 counties (Hancock, Harrison, Jackson) are wind-borne debris region; impact glazing or shutters required
- **Frost depth**: Minimal — 0-6" (essentially frost-free south MS); 6-12" north MS
- **Crawl space**: Moisture management critical — high humidity; vapor barrier mandatory

## Seismic / Wind / Natural Hazards

### Seismic
| Region | SDC | Notes |
|--------|-----|-------|
| Gulf Coast / south MS | A | Very low risk |
| Central MS (Jackson) | A | Very low risk |
| Northeast MS (Corinth area) | B | New Madrid Seismic Zone proximity |

- **New Madrid proximity**: Extreme northeast MS in zone of potential strong shaking

### Hurricane (Critical for Coastal MS)
| Region | Design Wind Speed | Notes |
|--------|-----------------|-------|
| Gulf Coast barrier islands | 150+ mph | Camille 1969 / Katrina 2005 |
| Biloxi / Gulfport coast | 140-150 mph | Direct Gulf exposure |
| Hattiesburg (60 mi inland) | 110-120 mph | |
| Jackson (150 mi inland) | 100-105 mph | |

- **Hurricane Katrina (2005)**: Category 5 landfall near Waveland — 28-ft storm surge; entire coast devastated; post-Katrina code updates required
- **Hurricane Camille (1969)**: 201 mph winds at landfall — benchmark event for Gulf Coast design
- **Post-Katrina IBC/ASCE 7 updates**: Major revisions to wind and flood provisions affected all Gulf Coast states
- **FEMA maps updated dramatically** post-Katrina; many properties moved into VE zones

### Tornado
- **Dixie Alley**: Mississippi is in the high-risk tornado corridor
- **2011 Smithville EF5**: Killed 15; rural northeast MS
- Mississippi averages 25-30 tornadoes/year

## Notable State Amendments
- **Post-Katrina coastal requirements**: Entire coastal zone rebuilt to higher standards
- **Biloxi / Gulfport**: Strong enforcement for coastal construction; verify Harrison County AHJ
- **Jackson**: Hinds County — largest city; local enforcement
- **Termite guarantee**: Many lenders require termite bond/warranty in south MS
- **MDEQ**: Mississippi Dept of Environmental Quality — coastal development, wetland permits

## Quick Checklist - New Residential
- [ ] Climate zone (2A coast / 3A north-central MS)
- [ ] **Termite treatment specified (mandatory statewide)**
- [ ] Coastal? Wind 140-150 mph; impact glazing; FEMA flood elevation (post-Katrina maps)
- [ ] Northeast MS? Verify New Madrid seismic influence
- [ ] SHGC control (Gulf Coast cooling-dominant)
- [ ] Crawl space vapor management (high humidity)
