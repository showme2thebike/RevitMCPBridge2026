## Energy Code

**Adopted**: IECC 2018

**Climate Zones**:
| Region | Climate Zone | Cities |
|--------|-------------|--------|
| Southern ND (Fargo, Bismarck) | 6A (cold-humid) | Fargo, Grand Forks, Bismarck, Mandan |
| Northern ND (Minot, Williston) | 7 (very cold) | Minot, Williston, Devils Lake, Rugby |

**Zone 6A Residential Envelope Minimums (IECC 2018)**:
- Ceiling/attic: R-49
- Wood-frame walls: R-20 + R-5ci or R-13 + R-10ci
- Mass walls: R-11.4/R-16.4
- Floors over unconditioned space: R-30
- Slab edge: R-15 for 4 ft
- Windows: U-0.30, SHGC no requirement

**Zone 7 Residential Envelope Minimums (IECC 2018)**:
- Ceiling/attic: R-49
- Wood-frame walls: R-20 + R-5ci or R-13 + R-10ci
- Mass walls: R-11.4/R-16.4
- Floors over unconditioned space: R-38
- Slab edge: R-15 for 4 ft
- Windows: U-0.28, SHGC no requirement

**Key Energy Notes for Extreme Cold Climate**:
- Continuous insulation (ci) is increasingly required to keep sheathing above dew point
- Vapor retarder: Class I (polyethylene film) typically required on warm-in-winter face of framing in Zone 6–7
- Blower door air leakage: ≤3.0 ACH50 required (Zone 6–7 same as Zone 5)
- Duct sealing required; ductwork inside conditioned space preferred
- Triple-pane windows common in Zone 7 projects despite only U-0.28 requirement
- Mechanical ventilation (ASHRAE 62.2 or HRV/ERV) critical — tight construction without adequate ventilation causes IAQ problems and condensation

**Heat Recovery Ventilators (HRV)**:
- Strongly recommended; increasingly required by energy-conscious builders
- In Zone 7, ERV or HRV is essentially mandatory for proper moisture and IAQ management

## Residential Code

**Adopted**: IRC 2018

**Key IRC Provisions for North Dakota**:
- Minimum ceiling height: 7'-0" habitable space
- Stair riser max: 7-3/4", tread min: 10"
- Egress window: 5.7 sq ft net clear, 20" wide min, 24" high min, sill max 44" AFF
- Smoke alarms: each sleeping room, outside each sleeping area, each story
- Carbon monoxide alarms: required with fuel-fired appliances or attached garage
- Garage separation: 1/2" gypsum on garage-side wall; fire-rated door

**Residential Sprinklers**:
- No statewide mandate for one- and two-family dwellings

## Seismic / Wind / Natural Hazards

### Seismic

North Dakota has **very low seismic risk**.

| Region | Seismic Design Category | Notes |
|--------|------------------------|-------|
| Statewide | SDC A | No special seismic detailing |

### Wind Design

**North Dakota's northern Great Plains location and extreme open terrain produce very high effective wind pressures. Most of the state is Exposure Category C.**

| Region | Basic Wind Speed (Vult) | Notes |
|--------|------------------------|-------|
| Western ND (Williston, Dickinson) | 105–115 mph | Exposure C; very flat open terrain |
| Central ND (Bismarck) | 105–110 mph | Exposure C |
| Eastern ND (Fargo, Grand Forks) | 100–110 mph | Exposure B/C |

**Design Requirements**:
- ASCE 7-16 wind design
- Exposure C default for rural and suburban areas
- Roof-to-wall connections critical
- Gable end bracing for wood-frame construction
- Garage doors: pressure-rated per wind design

**Tornado Risk**:
- North Dakota is at the northern edge of tornado activity — less frequent than Kansas/Nebraska but tornadoes do occur
- No statewide residential tornado shelter mandate
- ICC 500 / FEMA P-361 applicable if shelter is desired

### Snow Loads — Very High

**North Dakota has among the highest snow loads in the contiguous United States. Roof structural design for snow is a primary concern.**

| Region | Ground Snow Load (Pg) |
|--------|----------------------|
| Fargo / southeast ND | 35–40 psf |
| Bismarck / central ND | 30–35 psf |
| Minot / north-central | 35–45 psf |
| Williston / northwest | 25–35 psf |
| Devils Lake / northeast | 40–50 psf |

**Critical Snow Design Issues**:
- Drift loads on low-slope roofs and adjacent to parapets — ASCE 7 Chapter 7 required
- Unbalanced loads on sloped roofs
- Sliding snow off steeply pitched roofs onto walkways — design consideration
- Roof live load reduction does not apply when snow governs
- Heated structures: heat loss through roof melts base layer; ice dam formation at eaves — proper insulation/air barrier critical
- Unheated structures (garages, ag buildings): full snow load governs; no heat loss benefit

### Frost Depth — Critical

**This is one of the most important design parameters for North Dakota residential construction.**

| Region | Minimum Frost Depth |
|--------|-------------------|
| Fargo / southeast | 48 inches |
| Bismarck / central | 52–56 inches |
| Minot / north | 56–60 inches |
| Williston / northwest | 54–60 inches |
| Devils Lake / northeast | 56–60 inches |

**Foundation Requirements**:
- All footings and foundation walls must extend below frost line
- Slab-on-grade with heated building: insulated slab edge (R-15 for 4 ft per IECC)
- Unheated slab (garage): frost-protected or supported on footings below frost depth
- Frost-Protected Shallow Foundations (FPSF) per IRC Appendix F — viable alternative with continuous sub-slab and perimeter insulation
- Crawlspace construction: must address extreme cold with adequate insulation of floor or foundation walls

### Radon — Zone 1 Statewide

**All of North Dakota is EPA Radon Zone 1 (highest risk category — predicted average >4 pCi/L).**

**Radon-Resistant New Construction (RRNC)**:
- Gas-permeable layer under slab: 4" gravel or equivalent
- 6-mil polyethylene vapor barrier on sub-slab material
- All sub-slab penetrations sealed with appropriate sealant
- 3" or 4" PVC passive radon vent from sub-slab to above roof
- Electrical junction box in attic near vent for future active fan
- Post-construction testing recommended before occupancy

**Extreme Cold Interaction with Radon**:
- Stack effect is extreme in cold climates — passive radon vents may be more effective or may need active fan in very cold months
- Heated spaces: soil gas pressure differential greater in winter — more radon-resistant measures warranted

## Extreme Cold Climate Design

### Building Envelope Cold Climate Details

**Vapor Control — Critical in Zones 6–7**:
- Warm-in-winter face vapor retarder (poly film or vapor retarder paint)
- Continuous air barrier is equally or more important than vapor retarder
- Avoid creating vapor traps (double vapor barrier situations)
- Penetrations through vapor/air barrier must be sealed

**Thermal Bridging**:
- Steel framing in Zone 6–7 requires continuous exterior insulation to prevent condensation at thermal bridges
- Wood framing: R-5ci exterior insulation recommended (required by IECC for full compliance paths)
- Slab edge insulation: R-15 for 4 ft minimum

**Ice Dams**:
- Result from heat loss through attic melting snow at roof deck while eaves stay cold
- Prevention: adequate attic insulation (R-49 minimum), airtight ceiling plane, cold roof ventilation
- Note: code minimum R-49 may still be insufficient for tight air barriers without proper attic ventilation in Zone 7

**Mechanical Systems**:
- Furnace sizing for extreme design temperatures (Bismarck: -25°F design; Minot: -30°F design; Williston: -35°F design)
- Combustion air requirements for gas furnaces in tight envelopes
- Pipe insulation critical; avoid routing water lines in exterior walls
- Freeze protection: heat tape on exposed pipes; insulate where needed

## Notable State Amendments

1. **No statewide residential sprinkler mandate** — IRC R313 not adopted at state level
2. **Radon Zone 1 statewide** — RRNC on every residential project is standard practice
3. **Frost depth 48–60 inches** — must be confirmed with local jurisdiction for each project
4. **Snow load governs structural design** in most residential projects statewide
5. **Extreme cold design** — vapor control, HRV/ERV ventilation, and thermal bridging management are practical necessities even beyond code minimum
6. **Local adoption**: North Dakota state code serves as minimum; Fargo, Bismarck, and Grand Forks may have local amendments; always confirm with local AHJ
7. **IECC Zone 6/7**: Continuous insulation requirements are effectively mandatory for code-compliant walls

## Quick Checklist - New Residential

- [ ] Confirm climate zone (6A or 7) for project location
- [ ] IECC 2018 envelope values applied — ceiling R-49, walls with ci, floor, slab R-15/4ft, windows
- [ ] Frost depth confirmed with local AHJ (48–60" range)
- [ ] Foundation designed to frost depth; slab edge insulated per IECC
- [ ] Radon-resistant construction designed — gravel, vapor barrier, vent pipe, attic outlet rough-in
- [ ] Vapor retarder (Class I poly film) on warm-in-winter side of framing
- [ ] Air barrier continuity detailed throughout envelope
- [ ] HRV or ERV mechanical ventilation system designed
- [ ] Snow load per ASCE 7 Chapter 7; drift loads at roof transitions evaluated
- [ ] Roof structure sized for local ground snow load (35–50 psf range)
- [ ] Ice dam prevention: attic insulation, air sealing, ventilation
- [ ] Heating system sized for local design temperature (down to -35°F)
- [ ] Pipe routing avoids exterior walls; freeze protection where required
- [ ] Wind design per ASCE 7; Exposure C for most locations
- [ ] Smoke and CO alarms per IRC 2018
- [ ] Egress windows per IRC R310 in all sleeping rooms
- [ ] Verify local AHJ's adopted code edition and amendments
