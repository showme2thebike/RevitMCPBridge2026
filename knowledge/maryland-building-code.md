# Maryland Building Code - Key Amendments

**Based on**: Maryland Building Performance Standards, based on IBC 2018
**Base Code**: IBC 2018 with Maryland amendments
**Authority**: Maryland Department of Labor, Licensing and Regulation; county-level enforcement

---

| Requirement | Maryland | IBC 2021 |
|-------------|---------|----------|
| Code edition | IBC 2018 | IBC 2021 |

**Climate Zones**: 4A (most of MD — Baltimore, Annapolis, Frederick), 3A (southern MD / Eastern Shore tip)

| Element | Zone 3A | Zone 4A |

| Ceiling | R-38 | R-49 |
| Wall | R-13 | R-13+5ci |

### Maryland Energy Notes
- **EmPOWER Maryland**: State energy efficiency program incentivizes above-code performance

## Residential Code

**Adopted**: Maryland Residential Building Code, based on IRC 2018

### Key Amendments
- **Sprinklers**: Not required statewide for 1-2 family; **Montgomery County requires NFPA 13D for new residential**
- **Radon**: Zone 1 (Garrett County, Allegany County, Frederick County western portion — Appalachian region); Zone 2 (Baltimore metro, Annapolis); passive system required in Zone 1 counties; recommended Zone 2
- **Coastal**: Ocean City — FEMA AE/VE zones; post-Sandy updated flood maps; wind-borne debris region
- **Chesapeake Bay**: Extensive tidal flooding in waterfront communities; FEMA maps cover Chesapeake shores
- **Frost depth**: 30-36" western MD mountains; 24" Eastern Shore / Annapolis area

## Seismic / Wind / Natural Hazards

### Seismic
- SDC B statewide — moderate risk
- Standard provisions apply

### Wind / Hurricane
| Region | Design Wind Speed | Notes |
|--------|-----------------|-------|
| Ocean City / Assateague | 115-120 mph | Direct Atlantic exposure |
| Chesapeake Bay coast | 100-110 mph | Bay exposure + storm surge |
| Baltimore metro | 100-105 mph | |
| Western MD mountains | 95-100 mph | |

- **Post-Sandy (2012)**: Ocean City area flood maps updated; many Eastern Shore communities in expanded flood zones
- **1933 Chesapeake-Potomac Hurricane**: Historic event defining storm surge vulnerability of Bay region

### Flooding
- **Chesapeake Bay**: Extensive tidal flooding in Anne Arundel, Queen Anne's, Talbot counties
- **Baltimore Inner Harbor**: Tidal surge and nuisance flooding increasing
- **Potomac River**: DC suburbs — significant flood risk (Ellicott City flash flooding 2016 and 2018)
- **Ellicott City Historic District**: Catastrophic flash flooding twice in two years — tributary stream flash flooding model for urban design

---

## Notable State Amendments
- **Montgomery County**: Largest county; requires NFPA 13D residential sprinklers; strong local code enforcement
- **Baltimore City**: Separate code enforcement office; older building stock with lead paint and asbestos concerns
- **Ocean City**: Resort construction; strict coastal and wind requirements; verify Worcester County AHJ
- **Chesapeake Bay Critical Area**: 1000-foot buffer zones from tidal waters — significant development restrictions (Critical Area Commission permit)
- **MDE**: Maryland Dept of the Environment — wetland/waterway permits widely applicable

## Quick Checklist - New Residential
- [ ] Climate zone (3A Eastern Shore south / 4A most of MD)
- [ ] Montgomery County? Sprinklers REQUIRED (NFPA 13D)
- [ ] Radon passive system (Zone 1 western MD — required; Zone 2 — recommended)
- [ ] Chesapeake Bay Critical Area? Buffer zone permit
- [ ] Coastal? FEMA FIRM flood elevation; post-Sandy maps
- [ ] Frost depth (24-36" by location)
- [ ] MDE permits (wetlands, waterways)
