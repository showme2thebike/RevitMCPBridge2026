**Based on**: Montana Building Codes, based on IBC 2021
**Base Code**: IBC 2021 with Montana amendments
**Authority**: Montana Department of Labor and Industry (DLI)

| Requirement | Montana | IBC 2021 |
|-------------|---------|----------|

## Residential Code

**Adopted**: IRC 2021 with Montana amendments

### Key Amendments
- **Radon**: Zone 1 statewide — highest radon risk category; active soil depressurization required or strongly recommended
- **Snow**: Structural design critical — 30-80+ psf ground snow loads
- **Frost depth**: 36-48" depending on location
- **Wildfire**: Extensive WUI risk — forested areas throughout state
- **Sprinklers**: Not required; some municipalities adopt locally

---

## Seismic / Wind / Natural Hazards

### Seismic
- SDC B statewide — moderate risk
- Some fault zones (Intermountain Seismic Belt passes through western Montana)
- Helena, Missoula area: B-C

### Wildfire
- High risk in forested western Montana, eastern grasslands
- Gallatin, Missoula, Flathead, Lincoln counties — significant WUI
- Class A roofing, defensible space required in designated WUI zones

### Snow
| Region | Ground Snow Load |
|--------|----------------|
| Eastern plains | 20-30 psf |
| Billings / Great Falls | 25-35 psf |
| Missoula / Helena | 30-40 psf |
| Glacier area | 60-100+ psf |
| Mountain passes | 100-150+ psf |

### Radon (Critical)
- **Zone 1**: Entire state — highest EPA radon risk
- Montana has some of highest indoor radon levels in US
- Active soil depressurization strongly recommended for all new construction
