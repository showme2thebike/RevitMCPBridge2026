# Texas Building Code - Key Amendments

**Based on**: Texas has NO statewide commercial building code — local adoption varies
**Base Code**: Most cities adopt IBC 2015 or IBC 2018; some on IBC 2021
**Authority**: Texas Department of Licensing and Regulation (TDLR) for some building types; local jurisdictions for most commercial

---

## CRITICAL: Texas Code Structure

Texas is unique — **there is no statewide commercial building code**. Home-rule cities (populations > 5,000) adopt their own codes independently. This means:

- **Dallas**: IBC 2015 (with amendments)
- **Houston**: IBC 2015 (with amendments) — Houston has minimal zoning
- **Austin**: IBC 2021 (with amendments)
- **San Antonio**: IBC 2018 (with amendments)
- **Fort Worth**: IBC 2018 (with amendments)

**Always verify the specific edition adopted by the AHJ before starting a project.**

### TDLR State Jurisdiction (limited)
- Industrialized buildings (manufactured off-site)
- Accessibility for public buildings (Texas Accessibility Standards)
- Boilers and pressure vessels

## Residential Code

**Adopted**: IRC 2015 statewide (local amendments vary)
**Authority**: Local jurisdictions

### Key Amendments
- **Sprinklers**: Not required statewide for 1-2 family
- **Wind**: IRC wind provisions apply; coastal areas require engineered design
- **Termite**: Required in most of Texas — significant Formosan termite pressure (Gulf Coast)
- **Frost depth**: Varies 0" (south TX) to 18" (Panhandle)
- **Pier and beam**: Common foundation type — crawl space vapor control important

## Seismic / Wind / Natural Hazards

### Wind (Critical in Texas)

| Region | Design Wind Speed | Notes |
|--------|-----------------|-------|
| Galveston / Corpus Christi | 150-160 mph | Highest risk |
| Houston (coastal) | 130-140 mph | Wind-borne debris region |
| Beaumont / Port Arthur | 130-140 mph | |
| DFW / San Antonio | 110-115 mph | Standard |
| West Texas | 110-115 mph | High open terrain |
| Panhandle | 100-110 mph | |

- **Wind-borne debris region**: Within 1 mile of coast where V ≥ 130 mph; within hurricane inundation zone
- **Impact glazing / shutters**: Required in wind-borne debris regions
- **Hurricane straps / ties**: Required for coastal framing

### Seismic
- **Statewide**: SDC A — very low seismic risk
- **Induced seismicity**: North Texas (Azle, Irving area) has experienced induced seismicity from wastewater injection — monitor for local guidance
- Standard IBC seismic provisions apply

### Tornado (Critical)
- **Risk**: Texas has the most tornadoes of any state (150-200+ annually)
- **DFW Metroplex**: High risk urban area
- **Lubbock / Amarillo / "Tornado Alley"**: Extreme risk
- **No statewide shelter requirement**: However, some cities require safe rooms for schools, etc.
- **Best practice**: Include tornado shelter or safe room in residential design

### Flooding
- **Houston**: Extremely high flood risk (Harris County — most flood-prone county in US)
- **Post-Harvey**: Enhanced floodplain requirements in many Harris County jurisdictions
- **500-year floodplain**: Many Houston-area projects now regulated to 500-year standard
- **Flash flooding**: Significant risk Hill Country (Guadalupe, Blanco, Pedernales rivers)
- **Coastal**: FEMA V/AE zones — storm surge significant

### Heat / Energy
- **Summer design temperatures**: 95-102°F dry bulb in most of Texas
- **High humidity**: Gulf Coast — latent cooling load significant
- **Attic ventilation**: Critical — radiant barriers commonly used
- **West Texas**: Extreme temperature swings (-20°F to 115°F in Panhandle)

## Texas Accessibility Standards (TAS)

Texas uses its own accessibility standard (TAS), which is ADA-equivalent and enforced by TDLR:
- **Registered Accessibility Specialist (RAS)**: Required for review of public accommodations
- **TAS review**: Mandatory submittal for projects serving the public
- **TDLR registration**: Design professional must register accessibility documents

---

## Notable State Amendments

- **No statewide commercial code**: Always call the local building department first
- **Houston no-zoning**: Houston has no zoning code — deed restrictions govern land use
- **TAS / TDLR**: Mandatory accessibility review process unique to Texas
- **Post-Harvey flood standards**: Houston and Harris County have substantially enhanced flood requirements
- **Winter Storm Uri (2021)**: Building energy/mechanical code discussions ongoing for cold weather resilience
- **Solar**: Net metering and solar access laws — favorable for solar installation

## Quick Checklist - New Residential (Texas)

- [ ] Local AHJ verified — which IBC/IRC edition?
- [ ] Climate zone (2A/3A/3B/4B/5B)
- [ ] Energy code — IECC 2015 (residential statewide)
- [ ] Wind design — coastal? Impact glazing required?
- [ ] Flood zone — FEMA FIRM checked
- [ ] Houston project? Harris County enhanced flood standards
- [ ] Termite protection specified (coastal/East TX)
- [ ] Tornado shelter / safe room considered
- [ ] TAS accessibility review required? (public buildings)
- [ ] Foundation type — pier/beam vs slab; expansive soils?
