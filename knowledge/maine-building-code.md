# Maine Building Code - Key Amendments

**Based on**: Maine Uniform Building and Energy Code (MUBEC), based on IBC 2015
**Base Code**: IBC 2015 with Maine amendments
**Authority**: Maine Department of Public Safety, Office of State Fire Marshal; municipalities ≥4,000 population must enforce MUBEC

## Energy Code

**Adopted**: IECC 2021 (Maine proactively adopted current edition for energy)
**Climate Zones**: 6A (most of ME), 7 (Aroostook County / extreme northern ME)

| Element | Zone 6A | Zone 7 |
|---------|---------|--------|
| Ceiling | R-60 | R-60 |
| Wall | R-20+5ci | R-20+5ci |
| Floor | R-38 | R-38 |
| Windows | U-0.25 | U-0.20 |
| Air sealing | 3.0 ACH50 | 3.0 ACH50 |

### Maine Energy Highlights
- **Design temperature**: -10°F to -25°F; Caribou (Aroostook) averages among coldest in lower 48
- **HRV/ERV**: Mandatory for tight construction — cold climate requires heat recovery
- **Heating fuels**: Fuel oil historically dominant; heat pumps increasingly viable with cold-climate models
- **Efficiency Maine**: State program incentivizes above-code air sealing and insulation

## Residential Code

**Adopted**: Maine Residential Building Code, based on IRC 2015

### Key Amendments
- **Sprinklers**: Not required statewide for 1-2 family
- **Radon**: Zone 1 (central and western ME — granite and metamorphic bedrock); significant statewide; passive system strongly recommended
- **Snow**: Structural design critical — roof drift loads at parapets, unheated spaces, valleys
- **Frost depth**: 48" most of state; 60" Aroostook County (northern ME)
- **Ice dam**: Ice and water shield mandatory — 24" minimum from eave edge (IRC base); cold climate demands proper air sealing to prevent dam formation

## Seismic / Wind / Natural Hazards

### Seismic
- SDC B coastal/southern ME — moderate risk
- Periodic seismic activity (1940 ME earthquake M4.5 series)
- Standard provisions apply

### Snow (Critical)
| Region | Ground Snow Load |
|--------|----------------|
| Portland / southern coast | 40-50 psf |
| Augusta / central ME | 50-60 psf |
| Bangor area | 55-65 psf |
| Moosehead Lake / north-central | 70-90 psf |
| Aroostook County | 80-100 psf |

- **Drift loads**: Critical — parapets, lower roofs, valleys; unobstructed wind from open fields increases drift potential
- **Roof collapse**: Design for full ground snow load plus drift at all transition points
- **Ice dams**: Major issue — air sealing + insulation at roof-wall junction is essential

### Coastal
- **Atlantic coast exposure**: Maine's rocky coastline has significant wave and storm surge risk
- **Nor'easters**: Primary storm driver; 1978 Blizzard of '78 remains defining event
- FEMA flood maps apply to coastal and river floodplains

---

## Notable State Amendments
- **Small municipalities**: Towns under 4,000 may not have local code enforcement — contact state fire marshal
- **Rural construction**: Significant portion of ME has no local inspector; state authority applies
- **Portland**: Largest city; strong local enforcement + local energy overlay
- **Vacation/seasonal homes**: Large portion of housing stock; verify heating system design for winter vacancy

- [ ] Climate zone (6A most / 7 Aroostook County)

- [ ] Frost depth (48" south-central ME / 60" Aroostook)

- [ ] HRV/ERV — mechanical ventilation required for tight construction
- [ ] Radon passive system (Zone 1 — granite geology)
