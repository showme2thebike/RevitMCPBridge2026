# Kansas Building Code - Key Amendments

**Based on**: Kansas Building Code (KBC), adopting IBC 2018
**Base Code**: IBC 2018 with Kansas amendments
**Authority**: Kansas Division of Architectural Registration and Engineering (DARE); Kansas Department of Health and Environment (KDHE) for some occupancies

## Energy Code

**Adopted**: IECC 2018

**Climate Zones by Region**:
| Region | Climate Zone | Cities |
|--------|-------------|--------|
| Northeast / Kansas City metro | 4A (mixed-humid) | Kansas City, Topeka, Lawrence |
| North-central / Northwest | 5A (cool-humid) | Salina, Hays, Colby |
| South / Wichita area | 3A (warm-humid) | Wichita, Hutchinson, Dodge City |

**Zone 4A Residential Envelope Minimums (IECC 2018)**:
- Ceiling/attic: R-49
- Wood-frame walls: R-20 or R-13 + R-5ci
- Mass walls: R-8/R-13
- Floors over unconditioned space: R-19
- Slab edge: R-10 for 2 ft
- Windows: U-0.32, SHGC 0.40

**Zone 5A Residential Envelope Minimums (IECC 2018)**:
- Ceiling/attic: R-49
- Wood-frame walls: R-20 or R-13 + R-5ci
- Floors over unconditioned space: R-30
- Slab edge: R-10 for 2 ft
- Windows: U-0.30, SHGC no requirement

**Zone 3A Residential Envelope Minimums (IECC 2018)**:
- Ceiling/attic: R-38
- Wood-frame walls: R-20 or R-13 + R-5ci
- Floors over unconditioned space: R-19
- Slab edge: R-10 for 2 ft
- Windows: U-0.35, SHGC 0.25

**Key Energy Notes**:
- Air sealing: blower-door test or visual inspection at rough-in
- Duct sealing: leakage testing required (postconstruction or rough-in method)
- Mechanical ventilation: ASHRAE 62.2 for tighter homes
- Zone 3A: cooling load dominates; SHGC 0.25 limits solar heat gain

## Residential Code

**Adopted**: IRC 2018 (with local amendments by many jurisdictions)

**Key IRC Provisions for Kansas**:
- Minimum ceiling height: 7'-0" habitable space
- Stair riser max: 7-3/4", tread min: 10"
- Handrail height: 34"-38"
- Bedroom egress window: 5.7 sq ft net clear, 20" wide min, 24" high min, sill max 44" AFF
- Smoke alarms: each sleeping room, outside each sleeping area, each story
- Carbon monoxide alarms: required when fuel-fired appliances or attached garage present
- Garage separation: 1/2" gypsum on garage side of wall shared with dwelling

**Residential Sprinklers**:
- No statewide mandate for one- and two-family dwellings
- Some municipalities (Overland Park, Lenexa) have adopted local sprinkler requirements
- Verify with local AHJ

## Seismic / Wind / Natural Hazards

### Seismic

Generally low risk, but induced seismicity from oil and gas wastewater injection has elevated activity in south-central Kansas.

| Region | Seismic Design Category | Notes |
|--------|------------------------|-------|
| Most of Kansas | SDC A | Minimal seismic design requirements |
| South-central KS | SDC B (some areas) | Harper, Sumner, Reno, Barton counties |
| Induced seismicity area | SDC A-B | Monitor USGS for current hazard |

- SDC A: No special seismic detailing required for IRC residential
- SDC B: Anchor bolts and hold-downs per IRC Chapter 6 prescriptive

### Wind Design - CRITICAL

Kansas is the center of Tornado Alley. Wind design is the single most critical structural consideration for Kansas residential projects. Kansas ranks among the highest nationally for tornado frequency and intensity.

| Region | Basic Wind Speed (Vult) | Notes |
|--------|------------------------|-------|
| Western Kansas | 115-120 mph | High open plains; Exposure C |
| Central / Wichita | 120-130 mph | Peak tornado frequency corridor |
| Eastern / KC metro | 110-115 mph | Exposure B suburban areas |

**Design Requirements**:
- ASCE 7-16 wind load analysis; Exposure Category B or C
- Roof sheathing: increased nailing schedules at high wind zones
- Gable end bracing required for wood-frame construction
- Roof-to-wall connections: hurricane ties/straps recommended; increasingly required locally
- Garage doors: pressure-rated per wind design

**Tornado Shelter Requirements**:
- No statewide mandate for residential tornado shelters
- FEMA P-361 (safe rooms) and ICC 500 (storm shelters) are the applicable design standards
- Design wind: 250 mph per FEMA P-361 for safe rooms
- Many local jurisdictions (Wichita, Sedgwick County, Johnson County) encourage or incentivize safe rooms
- FEMA Hazard Mitigation Grant Program (HMGP) funding available for residential safe rooms
- Safe room materials: reinforced concrete or steel; typically below-grade or in-garage slab-on-grade

### Snow Loads

| Region | Ground Snow Load (Pg) |
|--------|----------------------|
| Western Kansas | 20-25 psf |
| Central Kansas | 20 psf |
| Eastern / KC metro | 20 psf |
| Northern Kansas | 25-30 psf |

- Relatively modest compared to northern states
- Drift and unbalanced loads per ASCE 7 Chapter 7

### Radon

Kansas Radon Zones (EPA):
| Zone | Counties | Risk |
|------|----------|------|
| Zone 1 (highest) | Northwestern KS: Thomas, Sherman, Cheyenne, Wallace, Greeley, Hamilton and surrounding | Greater than 4 pCi/L predicted |
| Zone 2 (moderate) | Central Kansas band | 2-4 pCi/L |
| Zone 3 (low) | Southeast Kansas | Less than 2 pCi/L |

**Radon-Resistant Construction**:
- Zone 1: Passive radon mitigation system required
  - Gravel sub-slab layer (4" minimum)
  - 6-mil polyethylene vapor barrier
  - Sealed penetrations
  - 3" minimum PVC pipe from sub-slab through roof
  - Electrical outlet in attic near vent for future fan
- Zone 2: Passive system recommended; some AHJs require
- KDHE recommends testing all new homes regardless of zone

## Notable State Amendments

1. No statewide residential sprinkler mandate - IRC Section R313 not enforced at state level; check local AHJ
2. Adoption lag: Kansas adopts IBC 2018; some jurisdictions lag or have local amendments
3. Local adoption: Kansas state code applies to unincorporated areas as minimum; cities/counties may adopt stricter standards
4. Kansas City metro: Johnson County and Wyandotte County have local amendments; coordinate with city building departments
5. Wichita: City of Wichita has its own amendments with high wind design emphasis
6. Manufactured housing: Governed by Kansas Manufactured Housing Act separately from IRC
7. Induced seismicity: Monitor USGS hazard maps for south-central KS before finalizing SDC

## Quick Checklist - New Residential

- [ ] Confirm climate zone (3A, 4A, or 5A) for energy compliance
- [ ] IECC 2018 envelope values met for zone - ceiling, walls, floor, slab, windows
- [ ] Air sealing and duct testing method selected
- [ ] Verify local AHJ residential sprinkler requirement
- [ ] Wind speed (Vult) determined for project location per ASCE 7-16 Figure 26.5-1B
- [ ] Roof-to-wall connections (hurricane straps) per wind design
- [ ] Garage door wind pressure rating confirmed
- [ ] Radon zone checked; passive radon system if Zone 1 (northwest KS)
- [ ] Seismic SDC determined; check induced seismicity hazard (south-central KS)
- [ ] Snow load per ASCE 7 Chapter 7
- [ ] Tornado safe room: consult owner; document FEMA P-361 if provided
- [ ] Smoke and CO alarms per IRC 2018
- [ ] Egress windows per IRC R310 in all sleeping rooms
- [ ] Verify adopted code edition with local AHJ (some cities lag or amend)
