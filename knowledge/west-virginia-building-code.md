## Residential Code

**Adopted**: West Virginia Residential Code, based on IRC 2018

### Key Amendments
- **Sprinklers**: Not required statewide for 1-2 family
- **Radon**: Zone 1 throughout significant portions of WV — shale and coal bearing geology; passive system strongly recommended; Zone 1 designation reflects real risk
- **Coal mine subsidence**: **Critical and unique** — extensive underground mine history throughout most of WV; special foundation requirements in mined areas
- **Snow**: Mountain areas significant — Snowshoe, Canaan Valley, Elkins area — structural design critical
- **Frost depth**: 24-36" varies by elevation; higher elevations 36-42"

---

## Seismic / Wind / Natural Hazards

### Seismic
- SDC B statewide — moderate risk
- Recent induced seismicity from oil/gas hydraulic fracturing in northern WV
- Standard provisions apply; monitor if wastewater injection increases seismicity

### Coal Mine Subsidence (Unique to WV)
- **Extensive underground coal mining**: Virtually all of southern and central WV has historical underground mining
- **Subsidence risk**: Surface can drop, crack, or collapse above abandoned mines
- **Foundation design**: Geotechnical investigation strongly recommended before design; consult WV Geological and Economic Survey mine maps
- **Shallow foundations**: At risk in subsidence areas; engineered solutions may be required
- **Mine Map Repository**: WV state maintains records; research before design

### Snow
| Region | Ground Snow Load |
|--------|----------------|
| Charleston / Huntington | 20-25 psf |
| Morgantown | 30-35 psf |
| Elkins / Randolph County | 40-50 psf |
| Snowshoe / Cheat Mountain | 50-70 psf |
| Canaan Valley | 50-60 psf |

- **Ski resort areas**: Snowshoe Mountain, Timberline — extreme snow design; roof drift critical

### Flooding
- **2016 Historic Flooding**: Record rainfall — multiple fatalities; widespread damage in southern WV (Kanawha, Nicholas, Greenbrier counties)
- **Gauley River, Greenbrier River**: Significant flood corridors
- **Elk River**: 2014 chemical spill — watershed awareness important

## Notable State Amendments
- **Charleston**: State capital; Kanawha County enforcement; local building department
- **Morgantown / Monongalia County**: WVU influence; growing; stronger local enforcement
- **Eastern Panhandle (Martinsburg/Charles Town)**: DC suburb growth area; most active building market
- **DEP**: WV Dept of Environmental Protection for mining and environmental permits
- **Oil/Gas construction**: Significant construction associated with Marcellus/Utica shale development

## Quick Checklist - New Residential
- [ ] Climate zone (4A most / 5A high elevations)
- [ ] Radon passive system (Zone 1 — strongly recommended statewide)
- [ ] Coal mine subsidence? Research mine maps; consider geotechnical investigation
- [ ] Mountain location? Snow load 40-70 psf range; drift design critical
- [ ] Frost depth (24-42" by elevation)
- [ ] Flood zone (FEMA FIRM — river corridor towns)
- [ ] DEP permits if near streams or disturbing significant area
