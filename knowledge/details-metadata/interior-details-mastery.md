## 1. CABINETRY & MILLWORK DETAILS

### 1.1 AWI Quality Grades (Architectural Woodwork Institute)

The AWI Standards (AWI 100-800 series) define three quality grades for all architectural woodwork:

| Grade | Use | Description |
|-------|-----|-------------|
| **Economy** | Utility, back-of-house, mechanical rooms | Minimum quality for materials, workmanship, and installation. Not intended for public view. |
| **Custom** | General commercial, residential | Mid-range quality. **Default grade if none specified.** Good balance of cost and appearance. |
| **Premium** | Executive areas, lobbies, boardrooms | Highest quality. Tightest tolerances, best material selection, finest craftsmanship. |

**Key AWI Standard Sections:**
- AWI 100: Submittal (shop drawing standards, material data, construction methods)
- AWI 200: Care and Storage
- AWI 300: Materials (lumber, panel cores, panel surfaces, veneers)
- AWI 400: Architectural Wood Casework
- AWI 500: Architectural Wood Paneling & Wainscot
- AWI 600: Architectural Wood Doors
- AWI 700: Standing & Running Trim
- AWI 800: Closet and Utility Shelving

**Tolerance Summary by Grade:**

| Measurement | Economy | Custom | Premium |
|-------------|---------|--------|---------|
| Face frame flush | 1/32" | 1/64" | 1/64" |
| Door/drawer gap | 3/32" | 1/16" | 1/16" |
| Warp (door) | 3/16" | 1/8" | 1/16" |
| Joint gaps | 1/32" | 1/64" | Hairline |

- Face frame: 3/4" x 1-1/2" hardwood (maple, oak, poplar)
- Box: 1/2" to 3/4" plywood or particleboard
- Back panel: 1/4" plywood or hardboard
- Bottom: 1/2" to 3/4" plywood or particleboard
- Shelves: 3/4" plywood or particleboard with edge banding

- Back panel: 1/4" to 1/2"
- All visible edges edgebanded (PVC, ABS, or wood veneer)
- 32mm system: Vertical rows of 5mm holes at 32mm spacing for adjustable shelves/hardware
- Full overlay doors with 0-3mm reveal

**Edge Profile Selection by Material:**

| Material | Common Edges | Notes |
|----------|-------------|-------|
| Granite | Eased, bullnose, ogee, dupont, mitered | Can handle complex profiles |
| Quartz | Eased, beveled, bullnose, ogee, mitered | Most versatile for profiles |
| Marble | Eased, beveled, ogee | Softer stone, simpler profiles |
| Laminate | Eased, beveled, post-formed bullnose | Limited by veneer nature |
| Solid Surface | Any profile | Thermoformable, seamless joints |
| Butcher Block | Eased, beveled, bullnose | Wood grain limits complex profiles |
| Concrete | Eased, bullnose, custom | Cast to any shape |

**Countertop Thickness:**
- Granite/Quartz: 3/4" (2cm) or 1-1/4" (3cm); 3cm is standard for kitchens
- Laminate: 1-1/2" total (3/4" particleboard + build-up + laminate)
- Solid Surface: 1/2" or 3/4" (may use build-up strips for thicker edge)
- Butcher Block: 1-1/2" to 3"
- Concrete: 1-1/2" to 3" (min 1-1/2" for reinforcement)

**Overhang Dimensions:**
- Standard front overhang: 1" to 1-1/2" beyond face of cabinet
- Bar/eating overhang: 12"-15" (requires support brackets at 24" o.c. max if > 10")
- Island overhang support: Corbels, brackets, or steel angle at 24"-36" o.c.
- Max unsupported overhang (stone): 10" for 3cm, 6" for 2cm

## 2. BATHROOM & WET AREA DETAILS

### 2.1 Shower Pan Waterproofing Systems

#### Traditional Mortar Bed (TCNA Method B414)
```
    SECTION THROUGH SHOWER FLOOR:

    Tile (3/8" typical)
    Thinset mortar (1/8")
    ________________________________
    Mortar bed (1"-1-1/2" thick)     <-- Reinforced with wire mesh
    ________________________________     Sloped 1/4" per foot to drain
    Waterproof membrane               <-- CPE or PVC liner (Oatey, etc.)
    ________________________________     Lapped up walls 3" above curb
    Pre-slope mortar bed (3/4"-1")   <-- 1/4" per foot slope to drain
    ________________________________
    Subfloor (3/4" plywood min)

    DRAIN DETAIL:
    - Two-piece clamping drain (Oatey)
    - Lower drain body set in pre-slope
    - Membrane clamped between flanges with bolts
    - Weep holes in upper drain body allow water
      trapped above membrane to reach drain
    - 2" P-trap below (IPC requirement)
```

#### Thin-Set Waterproofing (Schluter KERDI System)
```
    SECTION THROUGH SHOWER FLOOR:

    Tile (3/8" typical)
    Thinset mortar (unmodified) (1/8")
    ________________________________
    KERDI membrane (8 mil polyethylene) <-- Fleece-backed, bonded in thinset
    ________________________________       Waterproof + vapor retarder
    KERDI-SHOWER-T tray               <-- Pre-sloped EPS foam tray
    (or mortar bed sloped 1/4"/ft)         with integrated KERDI membrane
    ________________________________
    Thinset mortar bed
    ________________________________
    Subfloor (3/4" plywood min)

    SEAM/JOINT DETAILS:
    - KERDI-BAND: 5" wide strip for all seams, corners, and floor-wall transitions
    - Overlaps: 2" minimum at sheet seams
    - All set in unmodified thinset (Schluter SET, ALL-SET, or FAST-SET)
    - Penetrations: KERDI-SEAL-PS pipe seals, KERDI-SEAL-MV mixing valve seals
```

#### Liquid-Applied Membrane (RedGard, HydroBan, AquaDefense)
```
    SECTION THROUGH SHOWER FLOOR:

    Tile
    Thinset mortar
    ________________________________
    Liquid membrane (2 coats)        <-- Roller or spray applied
    ________________________________     Cures to monolithic membrane
    Cement backer board or mortar bed    Reinforced at corners with fabric mesh
    ________________________________
    Subfloor

    APPLICATION REQUIREMENTS:
    - Min 2 coats, perpendicular directions
    - Wet film thickness: 25-30 mils per coat
    - Dry film thickness: 15-20 mils total (measure with mil gauge)
    - Cure time: 12-24 hours between coats (until color changes)
    - Fabric mesh at all corners, changes of plane, and joints
    - Extend waterproofing 3" above showerhead height on walls
```

### 3.1 Stair Geometry Requirements

**IBC (Commercial) vs IRC (Residential) Comparison:**

| Dimension | IBC (Commercial) | IRC (Residential) |
|-----------|-------------------|---------------------|
| Max riser height | 7" (178mm) | 7-3/4" (196mm) |
| Min tread depth | 11" (279mm) | 10" (254mm) |
| Min width | 44" (>50 occ.) / 36" (<50 occ.) | 36" |
| Min headroom | 80" (6'-8") | 80" (6'-8") |
| Max nosing projection | 1-1/4" (32mm) | 1-1/4" (32mm) |
| Nosing radius | Max 9/16" (14mm) | Max 9/16" (14mm) |
| Landing depth | Min = stair width (max 48" required) | Min 36" |
| Max flight height | 12'-0" between landings | No limit (IRC) |
| Riser/tread uniformity | 3/8" max variation | 3/8" max variation |
| Winders | Not permitted in means of egress | Permitted with min 6" at walk line |
| Spiral stairs | Limited in means of egress | Min 26" clear width, 7-1/2" tread at 12" from narrow edge |

**Stair Geometry Formulas:**
- Standard rule: 2R + T = 24" to 25" (where R = rise, T = tread run)
- Comfort formula: R + T = 17" to 18"
- Safety formula: R x T = 70 to 75
- Common residential: 7-1/2" rise x 10" tread (typical 8' ceiling)
- Common commercial: 7" rise x 11" tread

```
    - Schluter RENO-T: Tile side embedded in thinset
    - Flush transition (best): Adjust underlayment thickness
      so both finishes end at same height

    HEIGHT CALCULATIONS:
    - 3/4" hardwood + 3/4" plywood = 1-1/2" total
    - 3/8" tile + 1/8" thinset + 1/2" cement board + 1/2" plywood = 1-1/2" total
    - With DITRA: 3/8" tile + 1/8" thinset + 1/8" DITRA + 3/4" plywood = 1-3/8"
```

```
    - F Rating: Time fire is prevented from passing through
    - T Rating: Time temperature stays below 325F above ambient
    - L Rating: Air leakage rate (CFM) - important for smoke protection

    PENETRATION TYPES AND SOLUTIONS:

    | Penetrant | Firestop Method | UL Test Standard |
    |-----------|-----------------|------------------|
    | Metal pipe (steel, copper) | Caulk sealant | ASTM E814 / UL 1479 |
    | Plastic pipe (PVC, CPVC, ABS) | Intumescent wrap strip + caulk | ASTM E814 / UL 1479 |
    | Insulated pipe | Mineral wool packing + caulk | ASTM E814 / UL 1479 |

    CRITICAL REQUIREMENTS:
    - Must use UL-listed system (not just any sealant)
    - System must match wall/floor rating (1-hr wall = 1-hr firestop)
    - Annular space per listing (typically 1/2" to 1" around pipe)
    - Plastic pipes: Intumescent material REQUIRED (pipe melts, intumescent closes gap)
    - Angle of penetration: 90 degrees (perpendicular) unless system is listed for angled
    - Max angled penetration: 45 degrees from perpendicular (if listed)
    - Inspection: All firestop installations subject to special inspection

    MAJOR FIRESTOP MANUFACTURERS:
    - 3M Fire Protection Products
    - Hilti Firestop
    - STI (Specified Technologies Inc.)
    - Tremco/Rectorseal
    - Nelson Firestop (now Emerson)
```
