# Washington DC Building Code - Key Requirements

**Based on**: DC Construction Codes 2020 (12th Edition), based on IBC 2018 / IRC 2018 with DC amendments
**Note**: DC adopted IBC 2018-based provisions as the 2020 DC Construction Codes; ongoing update cycle
**Authority**: DC Department of Buildings (DOB), formerly DCRA; DCRA oversees licensing

---

## Quick Reference - DC vs. IBC

| Requirement | Washington DC | IBC 2021 |
|-------------|--------------|----------|
| Code edition | DC 2020 (IBC 2018 base) | IBC 2021 |
| Energy code | DC Energy Conservation Code (IECC 2018 base + DC amendments) | IECC 2021 |
| Residential sprinklers | Required in most new multifamily | Not required |
| Seismic | SDC B | Per ASCE 7 |
| Green building | **Mandatory for large commercial** (DC Green Building Act) | Not addressed |
| Height limit | 130' max (Pennsylvania Ave: variable) | Not addressed |

## Energy Code

**Adopted**: DC Energy Conservation Code — based on IECC 2018 with aggressive DC amendments pushing performance toward IECC 2021+ standards
**Climate Zone**: 4A

| Element | Zone 4A (DC standard) |
|---------|----------------------|
| Ceiling | R-49 |
| Wall | R-20 or R-13+5ci |
| Floor | R-19 |
| Windows | U-0.32 |
| Air sealing | 3.0 ACH50 |

### DC Energy / Green Building
- **DC Green Building Act (2006, amended)**: Green building certification required for:
  - ALL new District-owned buildings: LEED Gold minimum
  - Private commercial >50,000 SF: LEED Certified minimum; recent amendments push toward higher
  - Residential ≥10 units: Green building requirements apply
- **Building Energy Performance Standards (BEPS)**: Existing large buildings must meet performance benchmarks — DC leads US in this area
- **DOEE**: DC Dept of Energy and Environment oversees green building compliance

## Residential Code

**Adopted**: DC Residential Code, based on IRC 2018 with DC amendments

### Key Amendments
- **Sprinklers**: Required for new construction ≥3 stories; 1-2 family detached typically exempt; verify with DOB — DC has several nuances
- **Frost depth**: 36"
- **Historic district**: Extensive historic districts throughout DC — HPO (Historic Preservation Office) review before permit
- **FAA height limit**: Maximum building height 130' (Pennsylvania Ave corridor near Capitol is different); enforced by Zoning Commission, not building code per se

## Seismic / Wind / Natural Hazards

### Seismic
- SDC B statewide — moderate risk
- Standard provisions apply

### Wind
- Design wind speed: 100-105 mph
- No hurricane direct landfall risk; tropical systems occasionally reach the area

### Flooding
- **Rock Creek**: Rock Creek Park corridor and lower reaches have significant flood risk
- **Anacostia River**: Eastern DC — flood plains; significant historic flooding; FEMA AE zones
- **Tidal**: Tidal Potomac influence at SW waterfront; post-Sandy flood awareness
- DOEE coordinates flood resilience planning

## Notable DC-Specific Requirements

### Height Act
- Buildings Act of 1910: Federal law limits building heights to 130' on most streets; 160' on Pennsylvania Ave (commercial)
- Does not apply to buildings west of Rock Creek or in residential areas differently
- No height exception exists for DC — unlike most US cities, you cannot get a variance

### Historic Preservation
- **50%+ of DC's land area**: In federal historic districts, local historic districts, or under historic review
- **HPO review**: Required before any permit for exterior work in historic districts
- **Capitol Hill Historic District, Georgetown Historic District, Dupont Circle**: Dense historic zones; strict design standards
- **Section 106**: Federal projects or projects using federal funds require Section 106 review

### Special Regulatory Bodies
- **NCPC (National Capital Planning Commission)**: Reviews federal projects in DC and federal lands in the region
- **CFA (Commission of Fine Arts)**: Reviews design for federal buildings and embassy row
- **DDOT**: DC Dept of Transportation for streetscape and right-of-way
- **WASA**: DC Water for utility permits

## Quick Checklist - New Construction in DC
- [ ] Climate zone: 4A
- [ ] Historic district? HPO review before permit
- [ ] Height limit: 130' max — confirm zoning compliance
- [ ] Large commercial? DC Green Building Act (green certification required)
- [ ] Green building certification path (LEED or equivalent)
- [ ] Flood zone (FEMA FIRM — Rock Creek, Anacostia corridors)
- [ ] NCPC review? (federal land or federal project)
- [ ] DOEE building energy benchmarking (BEPS for large buildings)

*Reference: DC Construction Codes 2020 (IBC 2018 base); DC Green Building Act*
*DC is one of the most regulated building environments in the US — historic review, federal oversight, and mandatory green building all apply to most significant projects*
*The 130-foot height limit is federal law — it cannot be waived by any local DC process*
