## Energy Code

**Adopted**: IECC 2018
**Climate Zones**: 5A (north IA — Iowa City north), 4A (south IA — Des Moines south)

| Element | Zone 4A | Zone 5A |
|---------|---------|---------|
| Ceiling | R-49 | R-49 |
| Wall | R-13+5ci | R-20 |
| Floor | R-19 | R-30 |
| Windows | U-0.32 | U-0.30 |

### Iowa Energy Notes
- Cold winters with -10°F to -20°F design temperatures
- Natural gas heating dominant
- HRV/ERV strongly recommended for tight construction

## Residential Code

**Adopted**: Iowa Residential Code, based on IRC 2018

### Key Amendments
- **Sprinklers**: Not required statewide for 1-2 family
- **Radon**: **Zone 1 statewide** — Iowa has among the highest average radon levels in the US; passive system STRONGLY recommended; many local health departments encourage active systems
- **Tornado**: Very high risk — no statewide enhanced construction requirements
- **Frost depth**: 42" north IA (Des Moines area); 36" south IA; 48" extreme north

---

## Seismic / Wind / Natural Hazards

### Seismic
- SDC A statewide — very low risk
- Standard provisions apply

### Tornado (Critical)
- **Tornado Alley border**: Iowa sits on the eastern edge of Tornado Alley and is high-risk
- **2008 Parkersburg EF5**: Killed 8, leveled the small town completely
- **2008 Iowa City EF2**: Struck during University of Iowa graduation weekend
- Iowa averages 35+ tornadoes/year
- No statewide enhanced tornado construction provisions

### Snow
| Region | Ground Snow Load |
|--------|----------------|
| Sioux City / northwest IA | 25-30 psf |
| Des Moines | 20-25 psf |
| Iowa City / Cedar Rapids | 20-25 psf |
| Dubuque / northeast IA | 25-30 psf |

- Blizzard risk significant — drift loads at parapets critical

### Radon (Critical)
- **Zone 1 statewide**: Iowa's geology (glacial till over uranium-bearing rock) creates very high radon potential
- **Average radon**: Iowa's average indoor radon is among the highest of any state
- EPA strongly recommends active mitigation in all Iowa homes

## Notable State Amendments
- **Des Moines**: Largest city; Polk County enforces locally
- **Cedar Rapids**: Second-largest city; 2008 historic flood — many properties rebuilt to elevated standards
- **Quad Cities**: Iowa side (Davenport/Bettendorf) coordinates with Illinois side; verify Rock Island County IL for cross-river projects
- **Iowa DOT**: Involvement for projects near state roads

## Quick Checklist - New Residential
- [ ] Climate zone (4A south / 5A north IA)
- [ ] **Radon passive system — Zone 1 statewide; strongly recommended as minimum; consider active**
- [ ] Frost depth (36-48" by location)
- [ ] Tornado risk — safe room considered
- [ ] Blizzard drift loads at parapets and lower roofs
- [ ] Flood zone (FEMA FIRM — Iowa River, Cedar River, Des Moines River corridors)
