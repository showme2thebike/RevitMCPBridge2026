## Residential Code

**Adopted**: Oklahoma Residential Code, based on IRC 2018

### Key Amendments
- **Sprinklers**: Not required statewide for 1-2 family
- **Tornado safe rooms**: Oklahoma strongly recommends FEMA P-320 safe rooms for new residential; some municipalities (Moore, Newcastle) require for new single-family construction
- **Wind**: Design for 100-115 mph; highest sustained design winds of any contiguous US state
- **Seismic**: Central OK — induced seismicity from wastewater injection; SDC B
- **Frost depth**: 18-24" north OK; 12" south OK
- **Radon**: Zone 2-3 (lower risk than midwest; verify county)

---

## Seismic / Wind / Natural Hazards

### Seismic (Induced Seismicity)
| Region | SDC | Notes |
|--------|-----|-------|
| All regions | B | Induced seismicity elevated statewide |

- **Induced seismicity**: Oklahoma went from ~1-2 M3+ earthquakes/year to 900+ in 2015 due to oil/gas wastewater injection
- **2011 Prague M5.6**: Largest induced earthquake in US history; damaged structures
- **2016 Pawnee M5.8**: Largest OK earthquake in recorded history
- Oklahoma Corporation Commission regulates wastewater disposal wells to reduce seismicity
- SDC B design requirements apply statewide

### Tornado (Highest Risk in US)
| Event | Year | Intensity | Notes |
|-------|------|-----------|-------|
| Oklahoma City F5 outbreak | May 1999 | F5 | Moore direct hit; 36 killed; 302 mph measured gust |
| Moore EF5 | May 2013 | EF5 | Same Moore corridor; 24 killed; 2.6-mile-wide path |
| Joplin MO (tracking into OK) | 2011 | EF5 | Originated in OK |

- **Tornado Alley epicenter**: Oklahoma City-Moore-Norman corridor is the highest-frequency, highest-intensity tornado zone in the world
- Oklahoma averages 55-60 tornadoes/year
- **Moore, OK**: Hit by F5 or stronger tornadoes in 1999, 2003, and 2013 — same community

### Wind (Critical)
- **Design wind speed**: 100-115 mph — highest sustained design wind requirement of any state in contiguous US
- **Exposure C**: Open terrain dominates — no reduction for suburban exposure in many areas
- **Roof design**: Hurricane straps, solid sheathing connections at all attachments

## Notable State Amendments
- **Moore, Newcastle**: Require tornado safe rooms for new single-family residential
- **Oklahoma City**: Strong local enforcement; verify Oklahoma County current adoption
- **Tulsa**: Second largest city; green initiatives growing
- **ODEQ**: Oklahoma Dept of Environmental Quality for environmental permits
- **Safe room resources**: FEMA P-320, FEMA P-361 — Oklahoma Emergency Management advocates actively
