# Vermont Building Code - Key Amendments

**Based on**: Vermont Fire and Building Safety Code, based on IBC 2018; Vermont Residential Building Energy Standards (RBES)
**Base Code**: IBC 2018 with Vermont amendments; IRC 2018 residential
**Authority**: Vermont Division of Fire Safety, Department of Public Safety

---

| Requirement | Vermont | IBC 2021 |
|-------------|---------|----------|
| Code edition | IBC 2018 | IBC 2021 |

## Energy Code

**Adopted**: Vermont Residential Building Energy Standards (RBES) for residential; Vermont Commercial Building Energy Standards (CBES) for commercial — among most progressive energy codes in US
**Climate Zones**: 6A (central/south VT — Burlington, Montpelier), 7 (Northeast Kingdom — St. Johnsbury, Newport)

| Element | Zone 6A | Zone 7 |
|---------|---------|--------|
| Ceiling | R-60 | R-60 |
| Wall | R-20+5ci | R-20+5ci |
| Floor | R-38 | R-38 |
| Windows | U-0.25 | U-0.20 |
| Air sealing | 3.0 ACH50 | 3.0 ACH50 |

### Vermont Energy Highlights
- **RBES/CBES**: Vermont-specific standards that exceed or match IECC 2021 in most areas
- **Blower door test**: Required at rough-in and final
- **HRV**: Effectively mandatory for tight construction; mechanical ventilation rate specified
- **Design temperature**: -10°F Burlington; -20°F to -25°F Northeast Kingdom
- **Vermont Energy Dashboard**: Statewide tracking of building energy performance

## Residential Code

**Adopted**: Vermont Residential Building Code, based on IRC 2018

### Key Amendments
- **Sprinklers**: Not required statewide for 1-2 family
- **Radon**: Zone 1 throughout most of VT — granite and metamorphic bedrock statewide creates very high radon potential; passive system strongly recommended; testing encouraged by VT Dept of Health
- **Snow**: Structural design absolutely critical — Green Mountains and Northeast Kingdom among heaviest in eastern US
- **Frost depth**: 48" south VT (Burlington area); 60" Northeast Kingdom
- **Ice dam**: Ice and water shield required; extreme cold climate demands perfect air sealing at roof-wall junction

## Seismic / Wind / Natural Hazards

### Seismic
- SDC B — moderate risk
- Some fault activity; Burlington area had M4.5 historically
- Standard provisions apply

### Snow (Critical)
| Region | Ground Snow Load |
|--------|----------------|
| Rutland / south VT | 50-60 psf |
| Burlington / Champlain Valley | 55-65 psf |
| Green Mountain peaks | 70-90 psf |
| Northeast Kingdom | 70-80 psf |
| Jay Peak / high elevations | 80-100+ psf |

- **Drift loads**: Critical at all roof level changes, parapets, valley intersections
- **Ice storms**: Frequent in VT — ice accumulation on roofs and tree branches; design for ice load

### Flooding (Post-Irene)
- **Hurricane Irene (2011)**: Catastrophic inland flooding — 3,000 homes destroyed; road network devastated; many Vermont rivers at 100-year+ flood levels
- **River Corridor regulations**: Vermont Act 138 (2012) and Act 110 (2014) established river corridor rules post-Irene; setbacks from rivers now regulated
- FEMA flood maps significantly updated in many VT communities post-Irene

---

## Notable State Amendments
- **Burlington**: Largest city; strong local enforcement; additional energy programs
- **Stowe / Sugarbush / Killington**: Ski resort construction; heavy snow design critical; mountain terrain snow studies required
- **Northeast Kingdom**: Rural; some towns have no local code officer; state authority applies
- **ANR**: Agency of Natural Resources — Act 250 (land use permit for larger projects); wetland permits
- **Act 250**: Vermont's major development permit for projects over 10 acres (or 1 acre in some municipalities) — requires natural resources review before building permit

## Quick Checklist - New Residential
- [ ] Climate zone (6A most / 7 Northeast Kingdom)
- [ ] **Snow load — structural design; drift at all roof transitions (50-100 psf range)**
- [ ] Frost depth (48" Burlington / 60" Northeast Kingdom)
- [ ] Ice and water shield (mandatory)
- [ ] HRV — mechanical ventilation required for RBES compliance
- [ ] Radon passive system (Zone 1 — granite statewide; strongly recommended)
- [ ] Blower door test required (rough-in + final)
- [ ] River corridor? Act 138/110 setback regulations
- [ ] Act 250 permit? (projects >10 acres or per local threshold)
