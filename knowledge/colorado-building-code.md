# Colorado Building Code - Key Amendments

**Based on**: Colorado adopts IBC 2021 at state level; local adoption varies significantly
**Base Code**: IBC 2021 (commercial) / IRC 2021 (residential) — locally adopted
**Authority**: Colorado Division of Fire Prevention and Control (state); local jurisdictions adopt independently

---

## Quick Reference - Colorado vs. IBC

| Requirement | Colorado | IBC 2021 |
|-------------|----------|----------|
| Code adoption | Local adoption — no single statewide commercial code | Nationally published |
| Energy code | ECPA (IECC 2021 base) — statewide residential | IECC 2021 |
| Residential sprinklers | Not required statewide; some jurisdictions require | Not required |
| Wildfire WUI | Significant — state WUI provisions | Not addressed |
| Radon | Zone 1 — highest risk statewide | Not addressed |
| Seismic | SDC A-B (mostly); SDC C Denver/Boulder area | Per ASCE 7 |

## Energy Code

**Adopted**: Colorado Energy Code (based on IECC 2021) — statewide for residential
**Authority**: Colorado Energy Office / local jurisdictions for commercial

### Climate Zones

| Zone | Region | Examples |
|------|--------|---------|
| 5B | Front Range / Denver metro | Denver, Boulder, Fort Collins, Colorado Springs |
| 6B | Mountains / high elevation | Aspen, Vail, Steamboat, Telluride |
| 7 | Highest elevation communities | Some mountain towns above 8,000' |
| 3B | Southeast corner | La Junta, Trinidad (low elevation) |

## Residential Code

**Adopted**: IRC 2021 (locally adopted — most Front Range jurisdictions current)
**Authority**: Local jurisdictions

### Key Amendments
- **Radon**: Radon-resistant new construction (RRNC) required or strongly recommended — Colorado is Zone 1 (highest radon risk) statewide
- **Radon system**: Active soil depressurization rough-in required in most Front Range jurisdictions
- **Wildfire**: WUI code applies in mountain communities — strict construction requirements
- **Sprinklers**: Not statewide; some mountain communities require (wildfire risk mitigation)
- **Snow loads**: Critical in mountain areas — 40-150+ psf ground snow load depending on elevation/location
- **Frost depth**: 36" Front Range, deeper in mountains

## Seismic / Wind / Natural Hazards

### Seismic
| Region | Typical SDC | Notes |
|--------|-------------|-------|
| Denver / Boulder | B-C | Some active fault zones |
| Front Range general | A-B | Moderate risk |
| Mountains | A-B | Low seismic activity |
| Eastern plains | A | Very low risk |

### Wind
- Front Range: 100-115 mph design wind speeds (chinook winds significant)
- Eastern plains: 100-115 mph (open terrain, Tornado Alley edge)
- Mountain ridges: 130+ mph at high elevation
- Boulder/Estes Park corridor: Enhanced wind design for downslope winds

### Wildfire (Critical)
- **WUI Code**: Applies throughout mountain communities and foothills
- **Defensible space**: Required 100' (Zone 1) + 100' (Zone 2)
- **Class A roofing**: Required in WUI zones
- **Ember-resistant vents**: Required
- **Ignition-resistant construction**: Required per WUI code
- **High-risk counties**: Boulder, Jefferson, Larimer, El Paso, Teller, Gilpin

### Snow / Elevation
- **Front Range**: 30-40 psf ground snow load
- **Mountain communities**: 80-150+ psf — structural engineer required
- **Freeze-thaw**: Significant; 3000 psi concrete minimum with air entrainment
- **Altitude**: Denver at 5,280'; buildings above 7,000' need altitude-adjusted HVAC

### Radon (Critical)
- **Zone 1**: Entire state — highest EPA risk category
- **Average indoor levels**: Among highest in US
- **Required**: Active soil depressurization rough-in (most jurisdictions mandate)

---

## Notable State Amendments

- **Radon**: Zone 1 statewide — treat as mandatory regardless of local requirement
- **Wildfire**: Significant liability exposure without WUI compliance in foothills/mountains
- **Code adoption patchwork**: Denver, Boulder, Fort Collins, Colorado Springs all on different adoption cycles — always verify AHJ
- **High-altitude HVAC**: Gas appliances need high-altitude derating; heat pumps preferred above 8,000'
- **Mountain municipality codes**: Aspen, Vail, Telluride have significantly more stringent green building requirements

## Quick Checklist - New Residential

- [ ] Climate zone determined (5B/6B/7)
- [ ] Energy code compliance — IECC edition adopted by local jurisdiction
- [ ] Radon-resistant construction (Zone 1 — always include)
- [ ] WUI zone? — wildfire construction requirements
- [ ] Snow load determined — structural engineer for mountains
- [ ] Wind design — Front Range chinook, mountain ridges
- [ ] Frost depth — 36" Front Range minimum
- [ ] Local adoption verified — which IBC/IRC edition adopted?
- [ ] Altitude — HVAC derating above 7,000'
