# Wisconsin Building Code - Key Amendments

**Based on**: Wisconsin Commercial Building Code (IBC 2015); Wisconsin Uniform Dwelling Code (UDC) for residential
**Base Code**: IBC 2015 commercial; UDC residential (state-specific, not IRC)
**Authority**: Wisconsin Department of Safety and Professional Services (DSPS)

## Energy Code

**Adopted**: IECC 2015 (commercial); UDC energy chapter (residential — roughly equivalent to IECC 2015)
**Climate Zones**: 6A (north WI — Green Bay north, Upper Peninsula border), 5A (south WI — Madison, Milwaukee)

| Element | Zone 5A | Zone 6A |
|---------|---------|---------|
| Ceiling | R-49 | R-60 |
| Wall | R-20 | R-20+5ci |
| Floor | R-30 | R-38 |
| Windows | U-0.30 | U-0.25 |

### Wisconsin Energy Notes
- **Design temperature**: -10°F Milwaukee; -15°F to -20°F northern WI; -20°F Ashland/Superior area
- **HRV/ERV**: Required for tight construction — Zone 6A demands heat recovery
- **Focus on Energy**: State utility program for above-code incentives (WPSC, WEC, other utilities)

## Residential Code (UDC — Unique to Wisconsin)

**Wisconsin Uniform Dwelling Code (UDC)**: Wisconsin does NOT follow the IRC — the UDC is a state-specific residential code.

### UDC Key Points
- Applies to all 1-2 family dwellings and manufactured homes
- Administered by DSPS; local municipality enforces
- UDC energy chapter updated periodically; roughly equivalent to IECC 2015
- **UDC chapters**: Chapter 20 (site), 21 (foundations), 22 (framing), 23 (heating), 24 (electrical), 25 (plumbing)

### Key Amendments
- **Sprinklers**: Not required statewide under UDC
- **Radon**: **Zone 1 statewide** — Wisconsin geology (granitic rock + glacial deposits over uranium-bearing formations); passive radon-resistant new construction (RRNC) required per DSPS guidance; many municipalities require active mitigation
- **Frost depth**: 48" most of WI; 60" extreme northern WI (Douglas, Iron, Vilas counties)
- **Ice dam**: Ice and water shield required; cold climate demands air sealing at eave-wall junction

## Seismic / Wind / Natural Hazards

### Seismic
- SDC A statewide — very low risk
- Standard provisions apply

### Snow (Critical)
| Region | Ground Snow Load |
|--------|----------------|
| Milwaukee | 30 psf |
| Madison | 30-35 psf |
| Green Bay | 35-40 psf |
| Rhinelander | 40-50 psf |
| Ashland / Lake Superior shore | 50-60 psf (lake-effect) |
| Iron County / Vilas County | 50-65 psf |

- **Lake Superior effect**: Ashland, Bayfield, Superior — significant lake-effect snowfall
- **Lake Michigan effect**: Green Bay, Sheboygan — enhanced snowfall from winter lake winds
- **Drift loads**: Critical at parapets and roof transitions — blizzard drifts can exceed ground snow load

### Tornado
- Wisconsin averages 20+ tornadoes/year — risk concentrated in southwest and central WI
- No statewide enhanced tornado construction provisions

## Notable State Amendments
- **UDC is not IRC**: Designers from other states must learn the UDC structure; local inspectors enforce UDC not IRC prescriptive tables
- **Milwaukee**: Large city; City of Milwaukee has own code enforcement department and local amendments
- **Madison**: Dane County strong enforcement; UW campus has state building oversight (DSPS)
- **Door County / Bayfield Peninsula**: Resort construction; heavy snow design; verify local AHJ
- **DNR**: Wisconsin Dept of Natural Resources for shoreland/wetland permits; extensive shoreline regulation
- **Shoreland zoning**: Wisconsin Chapter NR 115 — setbacks from navigable waters; 75-foot minimum setback

## Quick Checklist - New Residential (UDC)
- [ ] Climate zone (5A south / 6A north WI)
- [ ] **Apply UDC, not IRC — verify correct code sections with DSPS**
- [ ] **Radon passive system — Zone 1 statewide; RRNC required**
- [ ] Frost depth (48" south / 60" north)
- [ ] Snow load (30-65 psf by location; lake-effect north)
- [ ] Ice and water shield (cold climate mandatory)
- [ ] HRV required in tight construction (Zone 6A)
- [ ] Shoreland setback? DNR Chapter NR 115 (75' min from navigable water)
- [ ] Tornado risk (southwest WI higher)
