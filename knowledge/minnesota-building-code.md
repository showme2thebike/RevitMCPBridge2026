## Energy Code

**Adopted**: Minnesota Energy Code based on IECC 2018 — among most stringent in US
**Climate Zones**: 6A (Twin Cities south), 7 (northern MN / Duluth)

| Element | Zone 6A | Zone 7 |
|---------|---------|--------|
| Ceiling | R-60 | R-60 |
| Wall | R-20+5ci | R-20+5ci |
| Floor | R-38 | R-38 |
| Windows | U-0.25 | U-0.20 |
| Air sealing | 3.0 ACH50 | 3.0 ACH50 |

### Minnesota Energy Highlights
- **Mechanical ventilation**: Required — ERV preferred in cold climate (heat recovery)
- **Heating systems**: Natural gas dominates; heat pumps increasingly viable with cold-climate models
- **Design temperature**: -20°F to -30°F for mechanical design (Duluth/northern MN)
- **HRV/ERV**: Energy recovery ventilator essentially mandatory for tight construction

## Residential Code

**Adopted**: Minnesota Residential Code, based on IRC 2018

### Key Amendments
- **Sprinklers**: Not required statewide for 1-2 family
- **Radon**: Zone 1 statewide — highest EPA risk; passive radon system REQUIRED for new residential in Minnesota (state mandate)
- **Frost depth**: 42" Twin Cities; 48-60" northern MN
- **Snow**: Structural design for 30-50+ psf; roofs must shed or support load
- **Ice dam**: Ice and water shield required — 24" from exterior wall

---

## Seismic / Wind / Natural Hazards

### Seismic
- SDC A statewide — extremely low risk
- Standard provisions apply

### Tornado
- **High risk**: Minnesota averages 45+ tornadoes/year
- Twin Cities metro, southern MN highest risk
- **1965 Palm Sunday outbreak**: 47 tornadoes in state
- No enhanced statewide tornado construction provisions

### Snow (Critical)
| Region | Ground Snow Load |
|--------|----------------|
| Twin Cities metro | 30-35 psf |
| Duluth / Lake Superior | 40-60 psf (lake-effect) |
| Northern MN | 40-50 psf |
| Boundary Waters area | 50+ psf |

- **Lake Superior effect**: Duluth receives enhanced snowfall; structural design critical
- **Drift loads**: Critical at parapets, lower roofs, valleys — design for accumulation
- **Ice dam**: Significant issue — proper air sealing + insulation prevents

### Extreme Cold
- Design temperature: -20°F (Minneapolis); -30°F (International Falls — "Icebox of the Nation")
- **Mechanical sizing**: Heat loss calculations at extreme temps
- **Pipe freeze**: All supply piping must be protected; exterior hose bibs require freeze-proof
- **Foundation**: Frost depth 42-60" — frost footings or insulated slab system

### Radon (Mandatory)
- **Zone 1 statewide**: All of Minnesota in highest EPA risk category
- **State mandate**: Passive radon system REQUIRED for new 1-2 family construction
- Active system recommended in higher-risk areas (central/southwestern MN)

## Notable State Amendments
- **Radon mandatory**: Passive system required — not optional in Minnesota
- **Minneapolis**: City has additional green building requirements and local amendments
- **Saint Paul**: Verify local amendments
- **MN Pollution Control Agency**: Environmental permits often involved in development
- **DNR shoreland rules**: Setbacks from lakes, rivers, wetlands — strict regulation
