# Arkansas Building Code - Key Amendments

**Based on**: Arkansas Fire Prevention Code + local adoption of IBC 2018
**Base Code**: IBC 2018 (state agency buildings); local adoption for private projects
**Authority**: Arkansas State Fire Marshal; local jurisdictions for most private work

---

## Quick Reference - Arkansas vs. IBC

| Requirement | Arkansas | IBC 2021 |
|-------------|----------|----------|
| Code adoption | Local — no single statewide commercial code | N/A |
| Energy code | IECC 2015 (locally adopted) | IECC 2021 |

## Residential Code

**Adopted**: IRC 2018 — locally adopted; verify with AHJ

### Key Amendments
- **Sprinklers**: Not required statewide; some municipalities may adopt locally
- **Tornado**: High risk statewide — no statewide enhanced construction requirement; safe rooms encouraged
- **Radon**: Zone 2-3 — moderate risk; passive system recommended
- **New Madrid seismic**: Eastern Arkansas Mississippi Delta region — SDC B
- **Frost depth**: 18-24" north AR; 6-12" south AR

---

## Seismic / Wind / Natural Hazards

### Seismic
| Region | SDC | Notes |
|--------|-----|-------|
| Northwest AR (Fayetteville/Bentonville) | A | Very low risk |
| Central AR (Little Rock) | A | Low risk |
| Eastern AR (Delta / Jonesboro) | B | New Madrid Seismic Zone proximity |

- **New Madrid Zone**: Eastern Arkansas is within the New Madrid Seismic Zone influence area — M7+ possible
- Unreinforced masonry at risk in eastern AR

### Tornado (Critical)
- **Dixie Alley**: Arkansas sits squarely in the high-risk corridor
- **2011 Super Outbreak**: Multiple EF4-EF5 tornadoes tracked through Arkansas
- **2014 Vilonia EF4**: Directly hit the same town twice (2011 and 2014)
- Averages 30+ tornadoes/year
- No statewide enhanced tornado construction requirements

### Wind
- Design wind speed: 90-100 mph statewide
- Highest risk in central/eastern AR (open flat delta terrain)

### Flooding
- **Arkansas River**: Major flooding corridor through Little Rock
- **Mississippi River**: Eastern AR delta — regular flood events
- **2019 Historic Flooding**: Record Arkansas River levels in Little Rock and Fort Smith

## Notable State Amendments
- **Little Rock**: City has local building department; verify current AHJ adoption
- **Fayetteville/Bentonville**: Northwest AR rapid growth (Walmart HQ region); strong local enforcement
- **Jonesboro**: Eastern AR largest city; higher seismic awareness needed
- **No statewide residential code**: Many rural areas have minimal enforcement; verify county/city AHJ
