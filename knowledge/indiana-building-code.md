## Energy Code

**Adopted**: IECC 2014 — Indiana is notably behind on energy code adoption
**Climate Zones**: 5A (north IN — Fort Wayne, South Bend), 4A (central/south IN — Indianapolis, Evansville)

| Element | Zone 4A | Zone 5A |
|---------|---------|---------|
| Ceiling | R-49 | R-49 |
| Wall | R-13+5ci | R-20 |
| Floor | R-19 | R-30 |
| Windows | U-0.32 | U-0.30 |

### Indiana Energy Notes
- IECC 2014 is significantly less stringent than current IECC 2021
- Utility programs (Duke Energy Indiana, AES Indiana) often incentivize above-code performance
- Verify with AHJ — some municipalities may have adopted more recent editions locally

## Residential Code

**Adopted**: Indiana Residential Code, based on IRC 2014

### Key Amendments
- **Sprinklers**: Not required statewide for 1-2 family
- **Radon**: **Zone 1 statewide** — Indiana has very high radon levels; passive system strongly recommended; some local jurisdictions require
- **Tornado**: High risk — no statewide enhanced construction requirements; safe rooms encouraged
- **Frost depth**: 30" Indianapolis; 36" north IN (South Bend/Fort Wayne)
- **Wabash Valley seismic**: Southwestern IN near Evansville — SDC B; verify for larger structures

## Seismic / Wind / Natural Hazards

### Seismic
| Region | SDC | Notes |
|--------|-----|-------|
| Northern IN (South Bend) | A | Low risk |
| Indianapolis metro | A | Low risk |
| Southwestern IN (Evansville area) | B | Wabash Valley zone; New Madrid proximity |

- **Wabash Valley Seismic Zone**: Evansville area — higher risk than most of IN; 1968 M5.5 earthquake
- **New Madrid proximity**: Extreme southwestern IN within potential strong-shaking distance

### Tornado
- **High risk statewide** — Indiana averages 20+ tornadoes/year
- **1974 Super Outbreak**: Multiple F4-F5 tornadoes — Monticello and other IN cities devastated
- **2012 Henryville EF4**: Deadly outbreak across southern IN
- No statewide enhanced tornado construction standards

### Snow
| Region | Ground Snow Load |
|--------|----------------|
| South Bend / Michiana | 25-30 psf (Lake Michigan effect) |
| Fort Wayne | 25 psf |
| Indianapolis | 20-25 psf |
| Evansville | 15-20 psf |

- **Lake Michigan snow**: Northwestern IN (Gary/South Bend corridor) — significant lake-effect

## Notable State Amendments
- **Indianapolis**: Local code enforcement; verify Marion County current edition
- **Fort Wayne / South Bend / Evansville**: Each has active local building departments
- **IDEM**: Indiana Dept of Environmental Management for wetland/environmental permits
- **Code lag**: Indiana's IBC 2014 adoption means some design requirements differ significantly from current IBC — flag for out-of-state clients
