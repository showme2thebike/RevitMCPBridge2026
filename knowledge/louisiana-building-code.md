## Residential Code — POST-KATRINA AMENDMENTS (Critical)

Louisiana significantly strengthened its building code after Hurricane Katrina (2005). These amendments are critical:

### Wind Design
| Parish | Design Wind Speed | Notes |
|--------|-----------------|-------|
| Plaquemines / St. Bernard | 160-170 mph | Highest risk |
| Jefferson / Orleans (New Orleans) | 150-160 mph | |
| Terrebonne / Lafourche coast | 150-160 mph | |
| Baton Rouge | 120-130 mph | |
| Northern parishes | 110-115 mph | |

- **Wind-borne debris region**: Coastal parishes — impact glazing or approved shutters required
- **Continuous load path**: Required coastal — from roof to foundation
- **Product approvals**: Required for windows, doors, roofing coastal parishes

### Flood Design (Critical)
- **ASCE 24**: Required for flood-resistant design
- **Freeboard**: 1-2' above BFE required (varies by parish)
- **No-rise certification**: Required for floodplain development
- **V zones**: Storm surge — elevated construction required
- **Coastal parishes**: AE/VE zones dominate — nearly all land in flood zone

### Other Key Amendments
- **Termite**: Mandatory pre-construction soil treatment statewide — Formosan termites most destructive in US
- **Moisture**: Vapor retarder required; excellent drainage critical (high humidity)
- **Pile foundations**: Common in south Louisiana where soil is unsuitable for conventional footings

## Seismic / Wind / Natural Hazards

### Seismic
- SDC A-B statewide — low seismic risk
- Standard provisions apply

### Hurricane / Wind (Primary Hazard)
- Louisiana coast has highest hurricane frequency of any US state
- Major landfalls: Katrina (2005), Rita (2005), Ida (2021), Laura (2020)
- **Post-Katrina code**: 2006 reforms substantially elevated construction standards

### Flood (Primary Hazard)
- **New Orleans**: 50%+ below sea level; entire city in SFHA
- **Subsidence**: 1-3 cm/year in coastal areas — elevation certificates become outdated
- **FEMA maps**: Update frequently — verify current FIRM
- **NFIP**: Most properties in coastal parishes must carry flood insurance

### Extreme Heat / Humidity
- Design temperatures: 95°F dry bulb, 80°F wet bulb (New Orleans)
- **Attic design**: Ventilated attic critical; radiant barriers beneficial
- **Moisture intrusion**: Major issue — vapor barrier on warm side (outside in hot-humid climate)

## Notable State Amendments
- **New Orleans**: City of New Orleans has own building code office with local amendments
- **Post-Katrina recovery**: FEMA hazard mitigation grants available for elevation — verify eligibility
- **Manufactured housing**: Common in Louisiana — state has specific manufactured housing regulations
- **Industrial**: Significant industrial/petrochemical construction — own set of codes
- **Historic districts**: New Orleans French Quarter and Garden District — extensive HPO oversight

- [ ] Climate zone (2A most / 1A south coast)
