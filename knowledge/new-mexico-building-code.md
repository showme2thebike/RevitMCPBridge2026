**Base Code**: IBC 2018 with New Mexico amendments
**Authority**: New Mexico Construction Industries Division (CID), Regulation and Licensing Department

## Energy Code

**Adopted**: IECC 2018
**Climate Zones**: 4B (Santa Fe / Albuquerque / Taos highlands), 3B (Albuquerque warmer areas / central NM), 2B (southern NM — Las Cruces, Roswell, Carlsbad)

| Element | Zone 2B | Zone 3B | Zone 4B |
|---------|---------|---------|---------|
| Ceiling | R-38 | R-38 | R-49 |
| Wall | R-13 | R-13 | R-20 |
| Floor | R-13 | R-19 | R-19 |
| Windows | U-0.40, SHGC 0.25 | U-0.35, SHGC 0.25 | U-0.32 |

### New Mexico Energy Notes
- **Albuquerque**: 5,312' elevation — cooling AND heating significant; daily temperature swings 30-40°F
- **Santa Fe**: 7,000' elevation — cold winters (-10°F possible); hot summer days
- **Passive solar**: Traditional adobe design philosophy highly applicable; south-facing glazing
- **SHGC**: Critical in southern NM — limit solar heat gain in cooling season

## Residential Code

**Adopted**: New Mexico Residential Building Code, based on IRC 2018

### Key Amendments
- **Sprinklers**: Not required statewide for 1-2 family
- **Radon**: **Zone 1 (northern NM)** — uranium-bearing geology in Grants Mineral Belt and Jemez Mountains; very high risk; passive system required in many northern NM jurisdictions
- **Wildfire**: Northern NM — WUI provisions apply in forested areas (Jemez Mountains, Santa Fe National Forest, Taos)
- **Adobe/earthen construction**: Traditional building materials recognized; CID has specific earthen building provisions
- **Frost depth**: 24-36" Albuquerque; 36-42" Santa Fe/Taos; 12" Las Cruces/south

## Seismic / Wind / Natural Hazards

### Seismic
- SDC A-B statewide — generally low risk
- Some fault activity along Rio Grande Rift; standard provisions apply
- Albuquerque sits on the Rio Grande Rift but historically low seismicity

### Wildfire (Critical in Northern NM)
| Event | Year | Notes |
|-------|------|-------|
| Cerro Grande Fire | 2000 | Los Alamos National Lab threatened; 48,000 acres |
| Las Conchas Fire | 2011 | 156,000 acres — largest NM fire at time |
| Hermits Peak / Calf Canyon | 2022 | 341,000 acres — largest NM fire on record; 900+ structures destroyed |

- Northern NM highland communities (Mora, Las Vegas NM, Taos foothills) are extremely high wildfire risk
- **WUI requirements**: Chapter 7A equivalent provisions for new construction in interface zones
- **Defensible space**: Required around structures in WUI areas

### Wind / Dust
- **Haboobs**: Albuquerque and southern NM — significant dust storm events; wall of blowing sand
- **Design wind**: 90-100 mph statewide
- Strong afternoon thunderstorm winds spring-fall

### Extreme Temperature
- **Las Cruces**: Summer highs 100-105°F; winter lows 15-20°F
- **Albuquerque**: 106°F high; -17°F low (1971) — extreme range
- **Santa Fe**: Coldest state capital in US (-15°F recorded)

## Notable State Amendments
- **Albuquerque**: City of Albuquerque has own local amendments and strong enforcement
- **Santa Fe**: City has additional sustainability provisions; historic district review significant
- **Los Alamos County**: DOE oversight for nuclear facility proximity; specific local requirements
- **Taos Pueblo**: Tribal land — federal jurisdiction; contact BIA for tribal land projects
- **CID Earthen Building provisions**: Adobe, rammed earth, compressed earth block recognized construction types
